# HTK Outreach Operations Repository

## Purpose

This repository is the operating system for Virtual Assistants running manual X outreach for Hard To Kill Training (HTK).

The objective is simple:

1. Identify men who fit the HTK market.
2. Start relevant, human conversations.
3. Understand the prospect's goals and obstacles.
4. Confirm whether coaching is worth discussing.
5. Schedule qualified calls with Jimmy Bynum.

The VA does not sell, quote prices, diagnose injuries, prescribe training, or pressure prospects. The VA opens the right conversations and protects the brand while doing it.

## HTK Positioning

HTK is a premium performance and fitness coaching brand built by Jimmy Bynum, a former multi-sport athlete, Northern Michigan University football scholarship athlete, and United States Marine.

HTK is not bodybuilding coaching. HTK helps athletes, former athletes, military, veterans, first responders, fitness enthusiasts, and high performers develop:

- Explosiveness
- Endurance
- Mobility
- Athleticism
- Functional strength
- Durability
- Real-world performance

## Non-Negotiable Outreach Rules

- Every outreach action is performed manually by an authorized VA.
- Use one authentic HTK account. Do not create or use fake personas.
- Do not use browser scripts, bots, auto-DM tools, bulk senders, or engagement automation.
- Send only contextual messages to relevant prospects.
- Do not send identical DMs repeatedly. Use the approved script framework and adapt each message to the prospect.
- Do not send a link in the first message.
- Stop immediately when a prospect says no, asks to stop, or does not want messages.
- Never argue, shame, exaggerate, or make guarantees.
- Log every prospect and every meaningful action in the CRM.

## Daily Operating Standard

After the training ramp, the standard day is:

| KPI | Daily target | Daily cap |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| New personalized DMs sent | 20 | 25 |
| Follow-ups sent | 15 | 20 |
| New conversations started | 6 | Not capped |
| Qualified leads created | 2 | Not capped |
| Calls scheduled | 1 | Not capped |

Active two-way replies are handled promptly and do not count toward the new-DM or follow-up caps. The technical limit on X is not the operating target. Brand quality comes first.

## Your Role

You are the first human point of contact for many HTK prospects on X.

Your job is to:

1. Find men who fit the HTK market.
2. Start a real conversation.
3. Learn what they are training for and what is holding them back.
4. Decide whether HTK coaching is worth discussing.
5. Schedule qualified calls with Jimmy Bynum.
6. Keep the CRM accurate.

You do not close sales. You do not coach in DMs. You do not prescribe workouts. You do not diagnose injuries. You do not promise outcomes.

## What HTK Is

Hard To Kill Training helps men build performance that carries into real life.

HTK focuses on:

- Explosiveness
- Endurance
- Mobility
- Athleticism
- Functional strength
- Durability
- Real-world performance

HTK serves athletes, former athletes, military, veterans, first responders, fitness enthusiasts, and high performers.

HTK is not a bodybuilding program. A prospect can care about looking better, but the conversation should lead with performance.

## What Good Outreach Feels Like

Good outreach is:

- Specific to the person
- Short
- Easy to answer
- Curious without being intrusive
- Direct without being pushy
- Professional without sounding formal

Bad outreach is:

- A copy-pasted pitch
- A link dropped into the first message
- A long paragraph about HTK
- A fake compliment
- A message sent only because someone used a popular hashtag
- A conversation forced after the prospect has gone quiet

## The Daily Priority Order

Always work in this order:

1. Reply to active conversations.
2. Schedule prospects who have agreed to a call.
3. Send follow-ups that are due.
4. Research and log new prospects.
5. Send personalized first-contact DMs.
6. Update the CRM.
7. Submit the daily report.

A warm conversation is more valuable than sending one more first-contact message.

## Your Daily Numbers

Once your training ramp is complete:

| Activity | Daily target | Daily cap |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| New personalized DMs | 20 | 25 |
| Follow-ups | 15 | 20 |
| New conversations started | 6 | None |
| Qualified leads | 2 | None |
| Calls scheduled | 1 | None |

Do not chase the cap. The cap is a ceiling, not a goal. Never lower message quality to hit a number.

Use these points when a prospect asks what HTK is:

> HTK is performance coaching for men who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, and capable in real life. It is built around athleticism and durability, not bodybuilding-only training.

Use this when a prospect asks about Jimmy:

> Jimmy Bynum is the founder. His background includes multi-sport athletics, college football at Northern Michigan, and the Marine Corps. He built HTK around training that carries over outside the gym.

Do not add claims that are not in this handbook.

## Conversation Rules

- Ask one question at a time.
- Keep most messages to one or two short sentences.
- Use the prospect's language when describing their goal.
- Do not interrogate. Let the conversation breathe.

# Prospecting SOP

## Objective

Build a daily list of relevant men who are likely to value athletic, durable, real-world performance.

The VA researches manually. The VA does not scrape profiles, buy lead lists, use automated search tools, or send messages from a bulk sender.

## Daily Prospecting Target

Research enough prospects to send 20 high-quality first-contact DMs.

Recommended daily research:

| Activity | Target |
| --- | ---: |
| Profiles reviewed | 35-50 |
| New leads logged | 22-30 |
| First-contact DMs sent | 20 |

Expect to reject profiles. A smaller clean list is better than a large weak list.

## Ideal Prospect Signals

Prioritize profiles with at least two signals:

- Mentions a sport, athletic background, military service, veteran status, first-responder role, or performance training.
- Posts about strength, conditioning, running, mobility, recovery, durability, training consistency, or returning to athletic form.
- Follows or interacts with performance coaches, sports accounts, tactical fitness accounts, or training discussions.
- Expresses frustration with feeling slow, stiff, out of shape, inconsistent, or less athletic than before.
- Posts about preparing for a physical goal such as a season, tryout, race, fitness test, return to sport, or demanding work.
- Appears to be a man age 18-45 based on public profile context.

Do not guess at private traits. Use only public context.

## Priority Segments

| Priority | Segment | Common signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Athlete | Current sport, competition, team, season, tryout, measurable performance goal |
| 1 | Former athlete | Mentions prior sport, getting athletic again, stiffness, conditioning loss, or return to training |
| 1 | Military or veteran | Publicly states service and discusses fitness, readiness, durability, or training |
| 1 | First responder | Publicly states role and discusses training, readiness, or physical demands |
| 2 | Performance-focused fitness enthusiast | Trains consistently and values strength, conditioning, mobility, or performance |
| 2 | High performer | Wants a structured way to stay capable despite demanding work and schedule |

## Do Not Contact

Do not contact:

- Anyone who appears to be under 18.
- Anyone who has asked HTK not to contact them.
- Anyone already marked `Closed Lost` due to opt-out or rejection.
- Anyone with no credible fit to the HTK market.
- Private accounts with no public context that supports a relevant message.
- Obvious spam, bot, fake, or impersonation accounts.
- Accounts centered on explicit content, hate, harassment, or illegal activity.
- Prospects whose only signal is a trending topic or popular hashtag.
- Current HTK members unless the purpose is customer support and the manager assigns it.

## Manual Research Sources On X

Use these sources in order:

1. People who meaningfully interact with HTK posts.
2. Followers and following lists of relevant performance accounts.
3. Replies to useful discussions about athletic training, conditioning, mobility, durability, and returning to sport.
4. Manual X search for relevant phrases such as:
   - `former athlete training`
   - `getting athletic again`
   - `need better conditioning`
   - `training for fitness test`
   - `mobility work`
   - `return to sport`
   - `tactical fitness`
5. Relevant public profile bios discovered naturally during research.

Search is for discovery only. Do not message every person in search results.

## Prospect Fit Score

Score every prospect before sending a DM:

| Criterion | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Clear target-market identity | 0-2 |
| Public interest in performance training | 0-2 |
| Current training goal or frustration | 0-2 |
| Recent activity within 30 days | 0-1 |
| Natural conversation opener exists | 0-1 |

Decision:

| Score | Action |
| ---: | --- |
| 6-8 | Prioritize for outreach |
| 4-5 | Contact only if the opener is specific and relevant |
| 0-3 | Do not contact |

## Research And Contact Workflow

For each prospect:

1. Open the profile.
2. Read the bio.
3. Review at least five recent posts or replies when available.
4. Identify the segment.
5. Identify one real public signal.
6. Search the CRM by X handle and profile URL.
7. Skip the prospect if a record already exists unless a follow-up is due.
8. Calculate the fit score.
9. Log prospects scoring 4 or higher as `New Lead`.
10. Draft a contextual first-contact DM.
11. Send only if the message sounds natural and relevant.
12. Move the CRM record to `Contacted`.
13. Set Follow-Up 1 for three calendar days later.

## Personalization Note Standard

# Qualification Framework

## Purpose

The VA qualifies whether a conversation is worth moving to Jimmy. The VA does not conduct a sales call inside X DMs.

Qualification should feel like a useful conversation, not an interview.

## The Five-Part Qualification Gate

A lead becomes `Qualified` only when all five conditions are confirmed:

| Gate | What must be true | Example evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Market fit | Prospect belongs to an HTK segment | Former football player, veteran, first responder, athlete, or performance-focused lifter |
| 2. Performance goal | Prospect wants a performance outcome | Wants better conditioning, mobility, explosiveness, durability, or athleticism |
| 3. Meaningful obstacle | Prospect has a real gap or constraint | Inconsistent plan, stiffness, poor conditioning, schedule, or training without progress |
| 4. Coaching openness | Prospect is open to structured support | Says coaching, accountability, or a plan could help |
| 5. Call readiness | Prospect is willing to speak with Jimmy within 14 days | Agrees to a call and provides scheduling details |

Do not move a prospect to `Qualified` because he replied, was friendly, or likes fitness.

## Conversation Sequence

Do not send all questions at once. Ask only the next unanswered question.

### Question 1: Current Training

Use:

> What are you training for right now?

Alternative:

> What does your training look like these days?

### Question 2: Desired Outcome

Use:

> If you could improve one thing over the next few months, what would make the biggest difference: strength, conditioning, mobility, speed, or just feeling athletic again?

### Question 3: Main Obstacle

Use:

> What has been the main thing holding that back?

Alternative:

> Where do you feel the biggest gap right now?

### Question 4: Urgency

Use:

> Is that something you want to fix now, or are you mostly exploring ideas for later?

### Question 5: Coaching Openness

Use:

> Would you be open to a structured coaching plan if it matched what you are trying to build?

### Transition To Booking

Use only when the five-part gate is satisfied:

> Based on what you said, it sounds like a conversation with Jimmy would be useful. He can look at where you are now, what you are training for, and whether HTK is a fit. No pressure either way. Want me to set that up?

## Qualification Score

Use the score for reporting and prioritization:

| Category | 0 points | 1 point | 2 points |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Market fit | No clear fit | General fitness interest | Clear priority segment |
| Goal clarity | No goal | General improvement | Specific performance outcome |
| Obstacle | No gap stated | Mild frustration | Clear obstacle or cost of staying stuck |
| Urgency | Exploring later | Wants progress eventually | Wants action within 90 days |
| Coaching openness | Not interested | Unsure | Open to structured support |

Interpretation:

| Score | Action |
| ---: | --- |
| 8-10 | Invite to a call when all required gates are confirmed |
| 5-7 | Continue the conversation and clarify the missing gate |
| 0-4 | Do not push for a call; close respectfully or keep as a long-term relationship only when appropriate |

The score supports judgment. It does not override the five required gates.

## Disqualification Rules

Mark `Closed Lost` and record the reason when:

- Prospect says no or asks not to receive messages.
- Prospect wants bodybuilding-only or appearance-only coaching and rejects performance training.
- Prospect is under 18.
- Prospect wants free coaching in DMs and has no interest in a call.
- Prospect is not open to coaching.
- Prospect cannot or will not speak within the next 14 days after a clear invitation.
- Prospect is abusive, explicit, or clearly unsafe.
- Prospect is outside the target market and there is no clear HTK fit.

## Long-Term Relationship Rule

If the prospect is a fit but timing is not right:

1. Do not keep following up repeatedly.
2. Ask once:

> Understood. Want me to leave it there for now?

3. If the prospect says yes, mark `Closed Lost` with reason `Timing - prospect asked to pause`.
4. Do not contact again unless the prospect re-engages or the manager approves a specific relationship-based message.

## Sensitive Information

- Medical diagnosis
- Injury details beyond what the prospect voluntarily states
- Credit-card information
- Passwords
- Government ID
- Home address
- Income

For booking, collect only name, calendar email, time zone, and two available time windows.

- Next-action date

- Answers the opening question

- Shares a training goal
- Asks why HTK messaged

**Not meaningful:**

- Like
- Follow
- Emoji-only reaction
- Automated response
- Spam

**Move forward when:** The prospect discusses a goal, obstacle, or current training context.

**Definition:** The prospect is participating in a real two-way conversation and has shared enough context to begin qualification.

**Required CRM notes:**

- Current training
- Performance goal when known
- Obstacle when known
- Next qualification question

**Move forward when:** The prospect meets all qualification gates.

**Move to `Closed Lost` when:** The prospect declines, is not a fit, or asks to stop.

- Meaningful obstacle
- Coaching openness
- Willingness to speak with Jimmy within 14 days

- Segment
- Goal
- Obstacle
- Why now
- Coaching openness
- Qualification score
- Scheduling next action

**Move forward when:** The calendar invitation is sent and all call details are recorded.

- Time zone
- Calendar email
- Invite sent
- Reminder status
- Founder handoff summary

Confirm all seven:

- Public post
- Public reply
- Public interaction with HTK
- Clearly relevant public training context

Bad:
