# HTK VA QUICKSTART

## 1. Mission
- HTK is Hard To Kill Training: premium performance coaching for men who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, athletic, durable, and capable in real life.
- HTK is not bodybuilding-only coaching, medical advice, injury treatment, a free workout service, or a DM sales pitch.
- HTK serves men ages 18-45, especially athletes, former athletes, military, veterans, first responders, performance-focused fitness enthusiasts, and high performers.
- The VA is responsible for finding good-fit prospects on X, starting human conversations, qualifying goals and obstacles, booking qualified calls with Jimmy Bynum, keeping the CRM accurate, and protecting the HTK brand.
- The VA does not sell, close, quote pricing, prescribe workouts, diagnose injuries, promise outcomes, argue, pressure, or use automation.

## 2. Daily Workflow
1. Check X, CRM, warnings, unread DMs, due next actions, and calls in the next 48 hours.
2. Reply to active conversations first.
3. Escalate any medical, customer-service, public-figure, minor, security, or brand-risk issue.
4. Send due follow-ups only if the CRM says they are due.
5. Research new prospects manually.
6. Check the CRM for duplicates by X handle and profile URL.
7. Log good-fit prospects as `New Lead` before messaging.
8. Send personalized first-contact DMs manually.
9. Move sent leads to `Contacted` and set Follow-Up 1 for three calendar days later.
10. Qualify active conversations one question at a time.
11. Invite only qualified prospects to speak with Jimmy.
12. Schedule calls, update CRM, and add manager handoff notes.
13. Complete the daily report after confirming all touched leads are updated.

## 3. Prospect Selection Rules
**Green Flags (Good Prospects)**
- Publicly fits an HTK segment: athlete, former athlete, military, veteran, first responder, performance-focused fitness enthusiast, or high performer.
- Shows at least two real performance signals: sport, service, readiness, strength, conditioning, mobility, recovery, durability, return to sport, fitness test, race, tryout, demanding work, or wanting to feel athletic again.
- Appears to be a man age 18-45 based on public context and does not appear under 18.
- Recently active within 30 days.
- Has a natural, specific opener you can explain from public context.
- Fit score is 6-8.

**Yellow Flags (Possible Prospects)**
- Fit score is 4-5, and the opener is specific and relevant.
- General fitness interest, but some evidence of performance, mobility, durability, conditioning, or athletic goals.
- Busy professional/high performer who trains and wants structure, consistency, recovery, or capability.
- Prospect is a fit, but timing may be later. Do not pressure; clarify once.

**Red Flags (Do Not Contact)**
- Appears under 18.
- Private account with no public context for a relevant message.
- No credible HTK fit, or only signal is a hashtag/trending topic.
- Obvious spam, bot, fake, impersonation, explicit-content, hate, harassment, or illegal-activity account.
- Current HTK member unless assigned by manager for support.
- Already opted out, rejected HTK, closed due to opt-out, under 18, or unsafe conversation.
- Wants bodybuilding-only/appearance-only coaching and rejects performance training.

## 4. Outreach Rules
**What To Do**
- Research before messaging.
- Use only the authorized HTK account and approved access method.
- Create the CRM record before the first DM.
- Personalize from a real public signal: bio, post, reply, HTK interaction, or relevant training context.
- Keep most messages to one or two short sentences.
- Ask one easy-to-answer question at a time.
- Use approved scripts as frameworks, not copy-paste blasts.
- Follow up manually: Follow-Up 1 after 3 calendar days, Follow-Up 2 after 5 more days, Follow-Up 3 after 7 more days.
- Stop the no-reply sequence after Follow-Up 3.
- Reply once to opt-outs: "Understood. You will not receive another message from us."

**What Not To Do**
- Do not use bots, scrapers, browser scripts, auto-DM tools, bulk senders, fake personas, extra accounts, or engagement automation.
- Do not send links in the first message.
- Do not send identical DMs repeatedly.
- Do not message every follower, liker, search result, or hashtag user.
- Do not ask multiple qualification questions at once.
- Do not ask for medical, payment, password, government ID, home address, income, or unrelated private information.
- Do not diagnose injuries, prescribe workouts, give medical advice, guarantee results, shame, argue, use hype, or create false urgency.
- Do not continue after "no," "not interested," "stop," "no thanks," or any clear request to leave them alone.

## 5. Qualification Framework
A lead is qualified only when every box is checked:
- [ ] Fits the HTK target market.
- [ ] Has a performance-related goal.
- [ ] Has a meaningful obstacle, gap, constraint, or reason the goal matters.
- [ ] Wants progress now, not just vague future interest.
- [ ] Is open to coaching, structure, accountability, or a plan.
- [ ] Is willing to speak with Jimmy within 14 days.

Ask only the next unanswered question:
- Current training: "What are you training for right now?"
- Desired outcome: "If you could improve one thing over the next few months, what would make the biggest difference: strength, conditioning, mobility, speed, or just feeling athletic again?"
- Obstacle: "What has been the main thing holding that back?"
- Urgency: "Is that something you want to fix now, or are you mostly exploring ideas for later?"
- Coaching openness: "Would you be open to a structured coaching plan if it matched what you are trying to build?"

Do not qualify someone just because he replied, was friendly, likes fitness, or asks for a link.

## 6. Booking Process
Conversation -> Qualified -> Schedule Call -> CRM Update

1. Confirm all qualification gates are met.
2. Send: "Based on what you said, it sounds like a conversation with Jimmy would be useful. He can look at where you are now, what you are training for, and whether HTK is a fit. No pressure either way. Want me to set that up?"
3. If yes, collect only prospect name, calendar email, time zone, and two 30-minute windows in the next seven days.
4. Check Jimmy's approved availability.
5. Create a 30-minute calendar event titled `HTK Coaching Fit Call - Prospect Name`.
6. Add Jimmy and the prospect as attendees.
7. Use this event description: "HTK coaching fit call with Jimmy Bynum. We will discuss your current training, your performance goal, what has been holding progress back, and whether HTK coaching is the right next step."
8. Send the calendar invite.
9. Send confirmation DM: "You are set for the HTK coaching fit call with Jimmy. I sent the calendar invite to your email. Please accept it when you see it. If anything changes, message us here as soon as possible."
10. Update CRM with date, time, time zone, calendar email, invite status, and stage `Call Scheduled`.
11. Send manager handoff: name, X handle, call date/time/time zone, email, segment, goal, obstacle, why now, qualification score, and key notes.

## 7. CRM Rules
- The CRM is the source of truth. If it is not logged, it did not happen.
- Search by X handle and profile URL before creating any lead.
- Use only these stages: `New Lead`, `Contacted`, `Responded`, `Engaged`, `Qualified`, `Call Scheduled`, `Closed Won`, `Closed Lost`.
- Every touched lead must always have: `Lead_Stage`, `Last_Contact_Date`, `Last_Contact_Type`, `Next_Action`, `Next_Action_Date`, and factual `Notes` when context changed.
- Before first DM, record: `Lead_ID`, `Created_Date`, `X_Handle`, `Profile_URL`, `Segment`, `Public_Signal`, `Fit_Score`, `Lead_Stage`, `Next_Action`, `Next_Action_Date`.
- As discovered, update: `Goal`, `Obstacle`, `Why_Now`, `Coaching_Openness`, `Qualification_Score`.
- For scheduled calls, update: `Prospect_Name`, `Calendar_Email`, `Time_Zone`, `Call_Date`, `Call_Time`, invite sent, reminder status, and founder handoff summary.
- For closed leads, update: `Closed_Lost_Reason`, `Close_Date`, `Opt_Out`, and whether future contact is prohibited.
- Never store passwords, payment info, medical details, home addresses, government IDs, private DM content beyond operational notes, or guessed information.

## 8. Escalate Immediately If...
Stop replying, record the handle and factual summary, screenshot if appropriate, notify the manager, and wait for an approved response.

- Medical or injury question: prospect asks what to do, requests rehab/training advice for an injury, mentions diagnosis, pain, treatment, or medical limitations.
- Minor: prospect appears under 18 or says they are under 18.
- Public figure: professional athlete, large creator, media personality, organization account, partnership lead, sponsorship request, affiliate request, or media appearance.
- Customer complaint: refund, charge issue, billing claim, existing member issue, service complaint, or legal threat.
- Security concern: X warning, DM limitation, account restriction, unusual login, credential request, unexpected third-party tool, suspected unauthorized access.
- Brand-risk conversation: harassment, threats, explicit content, hate, politics/religion debate, public argument, negative public thread, competitor conflict, or any reply you are unsure protects HTK.
- Compliance incident: accidental private-information send, accidental unapproved link, multiple complaints in a short period, automation request, bypass-limit request, ignored opt-out, or duplicate bulk-message concern.

## 10. HTK Voice Guide
**DO**
- Direct
- Professional
- Human
- Disciplined
- Performance-driven
- Short and easy to answer

**DON'T**
- Sound salesy
- Send links first
- Use hype
- Make promises
- Shame the prospect
- Force military/tough-guy language
- Invent why you messaged

**5 Good Messages**
1. "Saw your post about strength being solid but conditioning falling off. What kind of conditioning are you trying to get back to?"
2. "You still training with an athletic focus, or has it turned into mostly strength work these days?"
3. "Looks like you are still putting real work in. What are you training for right now?"
4. "Saw the service background in your profile. Are you training more for strength these days, or keeping conditioning and durability in the mix too?"
5. "You mentioned consistency has been the issue when work gets busy. Would a structured plan help, or is the timing not right?"

**5 Bad Messages**
1. "Ready to transform your body? Book a call now: [link]"
2. "Jimmy personally picked you because you are perfect for HTK." 
3. "If you do HTK, we guarantee you will get faster and avoid injuries."
4. "Hey bro, you look out of shape. Let us fix that."
5. "Saw your hashtag. Want the best training program on the market?"
