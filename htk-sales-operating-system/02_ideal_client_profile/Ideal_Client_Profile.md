# 2. Ideal Client Profile

The closer must know the difference between an exciting prospect, a possible prospect, and a prospect who should not be sold.

### A-Tier Prospect

#### Demographics

- Man age 18-45.
- Athlete, former athlete, military, veteran, first responder, tactical professional, serious fitness enthusiast, or high-performing professional.
- Has enough schedule control to train consistently or is willing to build that control.
- Has the financial ability to invest in coaching without creating obvious distress.

#### Mindset

- Values performance over appearance-only training.
- Takes responsibility for his current condition.
- Wants structure, standards, and accountability.
- Respects coaching.
- Is coachable without needing to be coddled.
- Understands that results require effort over time.

#### Goals

- Get athletic again.
- Improve conditioning, mobility, explosiveness, durability, or functional strength.
- Prepare for sport, fitness test, season, race, selection, job demands, or real-life readiness.
- Stop feeling slow, stiff, inconsistent, or one-dimensional.
- Build a training system that fits his life.

#### Pain Points

- Strength exists but conditioning is poor.
- Training feels random.
- Mobility is limiting movement quality.
- Old athletic identity does not match current condition.
- Work schedule disrupts consistency.
- Effort is high but progress is unclear.
- Feels capable in the gym but not in life or sport.

#### Buying Likelihood

High.

The A-tier prospect can clearly connect his current limitation to a meaningful goal. He sees coaching as leverage, not a luxury. He is willing to make a decision when the fit is clear.

### B-Tier Prospect

#### Demographics

- Man age 18-45.
- General fitness enthusiast, recreational athlete, busy professional, former athlete with vague goals, or service-adjacent prospect.
- Public context or call context suggests some performance interest.

#### Mindset

- Interested in improvement but not fully clear on the cost of staying the same.
- May be coachable but needs more clarity.
- May value performance but still talks in general fitness terms.
- May be comparing several options.

#### Goals

- Get in better shape.
- Be more consistent.
- Improve strength and conditioning.
- Feel better day to day.
- Get back into a routine.
- Build confidence through training.

#### Pain Points

- Inconsistent schedule.
- Lack of structure.
- Training boredom.
- Low conditioning.
- General stiffness or frustration.
- Has tried programs but did not stick with them.

#### Buying Likelihood

Moderate.

The B-tier prospect can become qualified if the closer helps him define a performance goal, a real obstacle, and a reason to act now. Do not push to offer presentation until those pieces are clear.

### C-Tier Prospect

#### Demographics

- Outside target age range or unclear fit.
- Appearance-only bodybuilding prospect.
- Casual fitness browser.
- Prospect seeking free workouts, hacks, or motivational content.
- Prospect with no ability or willingness to commit.

#### Mindset

- Wants a shortcut.
- Avoids accountability.
- Blames outside factors only.
- Wants to be convinced rather than coached.
- Treats the call like free consulting.
- Has unrealistic expectations.

#### Goals

- "Get shredded fast."
- "Just tell me what workout to do."
- "I want to try it for free first."
- "I only want to get bigger."
- "I need guaranteed results."
- "I will do it when life slows down."

#### Pain Points

- Low commitment.
- Low urgency.
- Unclear goal.
- High skepticism with no openness.
- Wants outcomes without changing behavior.
- Wants medical or injury advice.

#### Buying Likelihood

Low, and often not worth selling.

The closer should not try to convert a C-tier prospect through pressure. Close respectfully, follow the CRM rules, and protect the brand.

## Quick Fit Test

A prospect is worth moving toward an offer when:

- He fits the HTK market.
- He has a performance goal.
- He has a meaningful obstacle.
- He cares enough to act.
- He is open to coaching.
- He understands effort is required.
- He can make a decision without being pressured.

If any of these are missing, continue discovery or disqualify.
