# 14. HTK Brand Protection Rules

Every closer represents HTK and Jimmy Bynum. Sales quality must never damage the brand.

## Claims Rules

The closer may say:

- HTK is performance coaching for men who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, athletic, durable, and capable in real life.
- HTK is built around athleticism and durability, not bodybuilding-only training.
- HTK coaching provides structure, programming direction, accountability, and adjustment around the client's goal.
- Jimmy Bynum is the founder and has a background in multi-sport athletics, college football at Northern Michigan, and the Marine Corps.

The closer must not say:

- HTK guarantees results.
- HTK prevents injury.
- HTK cures pain.
- HTK fixes medical conditions.
- HTK is the best, only, fastest, or guaranteed solution.
- The prospect was specially selected unless true and approved.
- Spots are disappearing unless manager confirms real capacity.
- Jimmy personally reviewed the prospect unless he actually did.

## Guarantees

No guarantees.

Approved language:

> I cannot guarantee an outcome. Results depend on the person, the plan, communication, consistency, and execution.

## Medical Advice

Closers must not:

- Diagnose.
- Recommend treatment.
- Tell prospect how to train through injury.
- Interpret pain.
- Replace medical clearance.
- Promise injury prevention.
- Suggest HTK can fix medical issues.

Approved language:

> I cannot give medical advice. If there is an injury or medical limitation, you should speak with a qualified healthcare professional before deciding how to train.

## Injury Discussions

Allowed:

- Ask whether a coach needs to be aware of anything.
- Ask whether prospect has been cleared to train.
- Document limitations the prospect voluntarily provides.

Not allowed:

- Diagnosing.
- Programming around an injury on the call.
- Saying HTK can rehab or fix the injury.
- Asking for detailed medical history beyond operational need.

## Refund Discussions

Closers must use only current approved policy language.

Do not:

- Invent refund terms.
- Promise exceptions.
- Suggest refunds are guaranteed.
- Argue about refund requests.
- Handle charge disputes emotionally.

If a refund or charge issue appears:

> I am going to flag this for the manager so it is handled correctly.

## Public Communications

Closers must not:

- Argue publicly.
- Share DM or call details publicly.
- Mock prospects, competitors, or clients.
- Debate politics, religion, or controversial topics from HTK accounts.
- Make medical claims in public.
- Use aggressive public pressure.

Approved public standard:

> Keep it professional, factual, and tied to performance.

## Escalate Immediately

Escalate when:

- Medical or injury advice is requested.
- Legal threat appears.
- Refund, charge, or customer complaint appears.
- Public figure, professional athlete, large creator, media, organization, partnership, sponsor, or affiliate context appears.
- Prospect is under 18.
- Prospect threatens, harasses, or sends explicit content.
- Prospect claims misrepresentation.
- Closer is unsure how to respond without brand risk.

## Final Test

Before saying it, ask:

> Would HTK be comfortable with this exact statement being shown publicly with the brand name attached?

If not clearly yes, do not say it.
