# 1. HTK Positioning

## What HTK Is

Hard To Kill Training is premium performance coaching for men who want strength, conditioning, mobility, athleticism, durability, and real-world capability.

HTK is built for men who want their training to carry outside the gym:

- Explosive movement
- Better conditioning
- More durable joints and tissues
- Improved mobility
- Functional strength
- Athleticism
- Physical readiness for sport, work, service, family, and demanding life

HTK is founded by Jimmy Bynum. Jimmy's background includes multi-sport athletics, college football at Northern Michigan, and the Marine Corps. The brand exists for men who want training that is useful, disciplined, and built for performance.

## What HTK Is Not

HTK is not:

- Bodybuilding-only coaching
- A physique-first transformation challenge
- A quick-fix program
- Free workouts in DMs
- Medical treatment, injury rehabilitation, or pain diagnosis
- A hype-based motivation program
- A generic gym plan
- A place for men who want results without accountability

Prospects can want to look better. That is normal. The closer must lead the conversation toward performance, athleticism, durability, and capability. If the prospect only wants bodybuilding or appearance-only coaching, HTK is likely not the right fit.

## Who HTK Serves

HTK serves men who value performance and are willing to train with discipline:

- Athletes
- Former athletes
- Military personnel
- Veterans
- First responders
- Fitness enthusiasts who care about performance
- High performers with demanding schedules
- Men who want to feel athletic, capable, and durable again

Primary age range: men 18-45. Do not enroll minors.

## Why Prospects Buy HTK

Qualified prospects buy HTK because they want a structured path to become more capable. They are usually not buying "fitness." They are buying a return to identity, performance, discipline, and confidence.

Common buying reasons:

- "I used to be athletic and I want that back."
- "I am strong but my conditioning is weak."
- "I feel stiff, slow, or beat up."
- "I need a plan that fits my work schedule."
- "I have been winging it and need structure."
- "I need accountability."
- "I need training that supports sport, service, readiness, or demanding work."
- "I am tired of training hard without becoming more capable."

## Core Transformation Offered

HTK helps the right client move from scattered or limited training to a structured performance system.

Before HTK:

- Training is inconsistent, one-dimensional, or improvised.
- Strength may be present, but conditioning, mobility, and athleticism are lagging.
- The prospect feels slower, stiffer, less durable, or less capable than he wants to be.
- The prospect knows effort is not the issue; direction and structure are.

After HTK, when the client does the work:

- Training has structure.
- The client knows what he is building and why.
- Strength, conditioning, mobility, and athleticism are developed together.
- The client has accountability and a plan built around the goal.
- The client is moving toward real-world performance, not just gym numbers.

## Positioning Language For Calls

Use this when asked what HTK is:

> HTK is performance coaching for men who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, athletic, and capable in real life. It is built around athleticism and durability, not bodybuilding-only training.

Use this when explaining who HTK is for:

> HTK is for men who want their training to carry over. Former athletes, active athletes, military, veterans, first responders, and high performers tend to understand it quickly because they care about being capable, not just looking like they train.

Use this when separating HTK from bodybuilding:

> Looking better can happen as a byproduct of better training and consistency, but HTK is not a bodybuilding-only program. The center of the work is performance: strength, conditioning, mobility, durability, and athleticism.

Use this when transitioning from discovery to offer:

> Based on what you told me, the gap is not effort. The gap is having the right structure around your goal and enough accountability to execute it consistently. That is where HTK can make sense.

Use this when the prospect is not a fit:

> From what you described, I do not think HTK is the cleanest fit right now. I would rather be straight with you than force the offer where it does not belong.

## Closer Standard

The closer must sound:

- Direct
- Professional
- Human
- Disciplined
- Performance-driven
- Calm

The closer must not sound:

- Salesy
- Desperate
- Hype-driven
- Condescending
- Overly familiar
