# 5. Discovery Call Framework

The HTK discovery call is a fit call. It is not a motivational speech, a free coaching session, or a pressure close.

## Call Objective

By the end of the call, the closer must know:

- Who the prospect is.
- What he wants to build.
- What is stopping him.
- Why it matters now.
- Whether HTK can help.
- Whether he is willing to execute.
- Whether he can make a decision.

## Call Structure

1. Opening
2. Rapport
3. Goal Discovery
4. Current Situation
5. Obstacles
6. Consequences of Inaction
7. Desired Outcome
8. Commitment Level
9. Transition to Offer
10. Decision

## 1. Opening

Purpose: Set the frame and make the call direct.

Script:

> Appreciate you taking the time. The goal of this call is simple: I want to understand where you are now, what you are trying to build, what has been getting in the way, and whether HTK coaching actually makes sense. If it is a fit, I will show you the next step. If it is not, I will tell you straight.

Questions:

- "Sound fair?"
- "How much time do you have set aside for this?"
- "Are you in a place where you can talk openly for the next few minutes?"

## 2. Rapport

Purpose: Create enough trust to have a real conversation without wasting the first 15 minutes.

Questions:

- "Where are you based?"
- "What does work look like for you right now?"
- "I saw you mentioned [sport/service/training context]. What was that background?"
- "How long have you been training consistently?"

Rule:

Keep rapport relevant to performance, training, service, work, or life context. Do not drift into politics, religion, gossip, or unrelated topics.

## 3. Goal Discovery

Purpose: Identify the performance target.

Questions:

- "What are you trying to build right now?"
- "If training was working the way you wanted, what would be different in 90 days?"
- "What matters most right now: strength, conditioning, mobility, speed, durability, or feeling athletic again?"
- "Is there a specific event, test, season, job demand, or personal standard you are training for?"
- "What would make you say, 'This was worth it'?"

Clarify:

- "When you say better conditioning, what does that need to let you do?"
- "When you say athletic again, what does that mean in your body?"
- "How would you measure progress?"

## 4. Current Situation

Purpose: Understand where the prospect is today.

Questions:

- "What does your training look like right now?"
- "How many days per week are you actually training?"
- "What are you doing for strength?"
- "What are you doing for conditioning?"
- "What are you doing for mobility?"
- "What are you doing consistently, and what keeps falling off?"
- "Are you following a plan or piecing it together?"

## 5. Obstacles

Purpose: Find the constraint that makes coaching relevant.

Questions:

- "What has been the main thing holding that goal back?"
- "Is the issue structure, time, consistency, recovery, pain, motivation, or not knowing what to prioritize?"
- "Where do you feel the biggest gap right now?"
- "What have you already tried?"
- "Why did that not solve it?"
- "What usually causes you to fall off?"

## 6. Consequences Of Inaction

Purpose: Confirm whether the problem matters enough to solve.

Questions:

- "If nothing changes for the next six months, what happens?"
- "What is the cost of staying where you are?"
- "How is this affecting sport, work, confidence, energy, or identity?"
- "What are you tired of dealing with?"
- "Why not just keep doing what you are doing now?"

Do not dramatize. Let the prospect tell the truth.

## 7. Desired Outcome

Purpose: Build a clear target state.

Questions:

- "What would a strong outcome look like?"
- "How do you want to feel in your body?"
- "What do you want to be able to do again?"
- "What would it mean to be more durable and capable?"
- "What would change if you had a clear plan and stayed consistent?"

## 8. Commitment Level

Purpose: Confirm whether the prospect will do the work.

Questions:

- "How serious are you about fixing this now?"
- "What are you willing to change to make training work?"
- "Can you commit to following a plan even when the week gets busy?"
- "Are you open to being coached and held accountable?"
- "If HTK is a fit, are you in a position to make a decision today?"
- "Is anyone else involved in that decision?"

## 9. Transition To Offer

Use only after fit is established.

Script:

> Based on what you told me, the issue is not that you are unwilling to work. The issue is that your training needs more structure around the goal, and you need accountability to execute it consistently. That is exactly where HTK can make sense. Want me to walk you through how it would work?

If the prospect says yes, present the offer.

If the prospect hesitates:

> Before I explain it, what feels unclear or off?

## Discovery Call Rules

- Ask one question at a time.
- Do not pitch before diagnosis.
- Do not interrupt the prospect's answer.
- Use the prospect's words when summarizing.
- Do not give medical advice.
- Do not design a training plan on the call.
- Do not skip disqualification.
- Do not present HTK if the prospect is not a fit.
