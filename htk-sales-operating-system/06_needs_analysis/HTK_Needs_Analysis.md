# 6. HTK Needs Analysis

The HTK Needs Analysis is the closer's diagnostic framework. It determines whether HTK coaching fits the prospect's goal, limitation, lifestyle, and commitment.

## Diagnostic Categories

1. Performance goals
2. Athletic background
3. Current limitations
4. Injury considerations
5. Lifestyle constraints
6. Commitment level

## 1. Performance Goals

Identify the primary target.

Approved HTK goal categories:

- Explosiveness
- Endurance
- Conditioning
- Mobility
- Athleticism
- Functional strength
- Durability
- Return to sport
- Tactical/job readiness
- Real-world capability
- Consistency around demanding life

Questions:

- "What are you training for right now?"
- "What do you want your body to be able to do?"
- "What needs to improve first?"
- "Is this goal tied to sport, work, service, family, confidence, or personal standards?"

Decision:

- If performance goal is clear, continue.
- If goal is general, clarify.
- If goal is appearance-only and prospect rejects performance, disqualify.

## 2. Athletic Background

Identify how the prospect relates to performance.

Questions:

- "Did you play sports growing up or compete at any level?"
- "What kind of training have you responded well to?"
- "When did you feel most athletic?"
- "What do you miss from that version of yourself?"
- "What training experience do you already have?"

Decision:

- Current or former athlete: likely strong fit if commitment exists.
- Military/first responder: strong fit if readiness/durability matters.
- Fitness enthusiast: fit depends on performance orientation.
- No training background: possible fit only if coachable and realistic.

## 3. Current Limitations

Find the gap.

Common limitations:

- Poor conditioning
- Stiffness
- Strength-only training
- Low speed or explosiveness
- Inconsistent schedule
- Lack of structure
- Poor recovery
- No accountability
- Training without measurable progress

Questions:

- "What is the biggest gap between where you are and where you want to be?"
- "What keeps breaking down?"
- "What do you avoid because it exposes the weakness?"
- "Where are you strongest right now?"
- "Where are you weakest right now?"

Decision:

- If limitation matches HTK coaching scope, continue.
- If limitation is medical or injury-specific, avoid advice and escalate if needed.
- If limitation is unwillingness to work, disqualify.

## 4. Injury Considerations

The closer must identify injury context without diagnosing or advising.

Allowed questions:

- "Is there anything a coach would need to be aware of before programming?"
- "Have you been cleared by a qualified healthcare professional to train?"
- "Are there movements or activities you have been told to avoid?"

Not allowed:

- "What do you think the injury is?"
- "Does it hurt when you do this?"
- "You should do this exercise."
- "HTK can fix that."
- "You probably have [diagnosis]."

Decision:

- Cleared and manageable: continue, document.
- Not cleared: do not sell until prospect gets medical clearance.
- Asking for diagnosis or rehab advice: stop and escalate.

## 5. Lifestyle Constraints

Determine whether the prospect can execute.

Questions:

- "What does a normal week look like?"
- "How many days can you realistically train?"
- "What has made consistency hard?"
- "What time of day works best for training?"
- "What support or accountability do you need?"
- "What would need to change for this to work?"

Decision:

- Has constraints but willing to plan: possible fit.
- Has constraints and refuses change: disqualify.
- Wants coaching to overcome schedule chaos but accepts accountability: strong fit.

## 6. Commitment Level

HTK requires action.

Questions:

- "On a 1-10, how serious are you about solving this now?"
- "Why that number?"
- "What would make it a 9 or 10?"
- "Are you open to being coached directly?"
- "Are you willing to follow the plan even when motivation dips?"
- "If this is the right fit, are you ready to start?"

Decision:

- 8-10 with clear reason: move to offer if other gates are met.
- 5-7: clarify obstacle or timing.
- 1-4: do not close; timing is likely wrong.

## Decision Trees

### Decision Tree 1: Performance Fit

Prospect has performance goal?

- Yes -> Identify limitation.
- General fitness only -> Ask what fitness needs to help him do.
- Appearance-only -> Explain HTK positioning.
- Appearance-only and rejects performance -> Disqualify.

### Decision Tree 2: Coaching Fit

Prospect open to coaching?

- Yes -> Continue to commitment.
- Unsure -> Ask what support he believes would help.
- No -> Do not sell. Close respectfully.

### Decision Tree 3: Commitment Fit

Prospect willing to execute?

- Yes -> Present offer when fit is clear.
- Says "maybe" -> Clarify what is missing.
- No schedule or effort willingness -> Disqualify.

### Decision Tree 4: Injury Context

Prospect mentions injury?

- Cleared to train and not asking for advice -> Document and continue carefully.
- Not cleared -> Recommend medical clearance before coaching decision.
- Asks what to do medically -> Stop, state no medical advice, escalate if needed.

### Decision Tree 5: Decision Readiness

Can prospect decide today?

- Yes -> Present offer and close clearly.
- Needs spouse/partner -> Clarify decision process and schedule exact follow-up.
- Wants indefinite time -> Set one follow-up. Do not chase indefinitely.
