# 3. Disqualification Framework

HTK does not sell every prospect. The closer protects the brand by disqualifying poor-fit prospects before they become poor-fit clients.

## When Not To Sell

Do not sell when the prospect:

- Wants bodybuilding-only or appearance-only coaching and rejects performance training.
- Wants a quick fix, hack, or shortcut.
- Refuses accountability.
- Expects results without training, tracking, communication, or schedule changes.
- Wants guaranteed results.
- Wants medical advice, injury diagnosis, pain treatment, or rehab programming.
- Is under 18.
- Is abusive, hostile, explicit, threatening, or disrespectful.
- Is looking for free coaching or a full plan on the call.
- Cannot explain why the goal matters.
- Has no willingness to follow a structured plan.
- Has no realistic ability to train consistently.
- Is not willing to communicate with the coach.
- Says the investment would create serious financial distress.
- Needs to consult a medical professional before training.
- Is outside HTK's target market and there is no performance-coaching fit.

## Disqualification Language

Use direct, respectful language:

> Based on what you described, I do not think HTK is the cleanest fit right now. I would rather be straight with you than force the offer where it does not belong.

For bodybuilding-only:

> If the main goal is appearance-only bodybuilding, HTK may not be the right fit. The coaching is built around performance, durability, conditioning, mobility, and athleticism.

For free coaching:

> I cannot build a training plan for you on this call. If you want coaching around the goal, we can talk through whether HTK is the right structure. If not, no problem.

For medical/injury:

> I cannot give medical advice or tell you how to train through an injury. You need to speak with a qualified healthcare professional before deciding on a training path.

For unrealistic expectations:

> I do not want to set the wrong expectation. HTK can give you structure and coaching, but it cannot replace the work. If you are not ready to execute consistently, it is probably not the right time.

## Red Flag Scorecard

Score each red flag present during the call.

| Red Flag | Points |
|---|---:|
| Bodybuilding-only goal and rejects performance training | 3 |
| Wants guaranteed outcome | 3 |
| Wants free coaching / full plan before enrolling | 2 |
| Refuses accountability or tracking | 3 |
| Unrealistic timeline | 2 |
| No meaningful goal | 1 |
| No meaningful obstacle | 1 |
| No willingness to change schedule or behavior | 3 |
| Hostile, disrespectful, explicit, or unsafe | 5 |
| Medical or injury advice request | 5 |
| Under 18 | 5 |
| Serious financial distress stated | 3 |
| Cannot make a decision and wants indefinite follow-up | 1 |
| Public figure, pro athlete, large creator, media, or partnership context | 2 and escalate |

## Score Interpretation

| Score | Action |
|---|---|
| 0-2 | Continue discovery. |
| 3-4 | Slow down. Clarify fit before presenting the offer. |
| 5-6 | Disqualify unless manager approves a specific reason to continue. |
| 7+ | Do not sell. Close respectfully and record the reason. |
| Any 5-point safety flag | Stop selling and escalate when required. |

## Required CRM Note

When disqualifying, record:

- Disqualification reason
- Exact prospect statement or behavior
- Whether future follow-up is allowed
- Whether manager escalation is required
- Final closer action
