# 9. Closing Framework

HTK closing is based on clarity, commitment, and fit.

The closer does not manipulate. The closer helps the prospect make a clean decision.

## Closing Principles

- Close only after discovery and offer presentation.
- Use the prospect's stated goal, obstacle, and consequence.
- Ask direct questions.
- Respect a clear no.
- Do not create false urgency.
- Do not discount without manager approval.
- Do not drag poor-fit prospects across the line.
- Do not close someone who does not understand the commitment.

## Fit-Based Close

Use when the prospect understands the offer and appears qualified.

Script:

> Based on what you told me, HTK fits the goal. You want [goal], the main thing holding it back is [obstacle], and you said you are ready to [commitment]. The next step is enrollment. Do you want to move forward?

If yes:

> Good. I will walk you through enrollment now.

If unsure:

> What is the part that still feels unclear?

## Commitment Close

Use when the prospect likes the offer but hesitates around execution.

Script:

> The coaching can give you structure and accountability, but it cannot do the work for you. Are you willing to follow the plan and communicate when the week gets difficult?

If yes:

> Then the question is whether you want to start solving this now.

If no:

> Then I do not think it is the right time. I would rather have you wait than enroll without the commitment.

## Clarity Close

Use when the prospect gives vague hesitation.

Script:

> Let's make this clear. Is the hesitation about fit, timing, investment, or whether you will follow through?

Then handle the real issue. Do not keep explaining the offer.

## Decision Close

Use when the prospect has all information and no remaining objection.

Script:

> You have the information. You know what you want to change. You know what HTK requires. Do you want to move forward and start?

## No-Pressure Close

Use when the prospect is qualified but cautious.

Script:

> No pressure either way. If this is the right fit, we can get you enrolled and moving. If it is not, I would rather know now. What is the honest answer?

## Disqualification Close

Use when HTK is not the right fit.

Script:

> I do not think HTK is the right fit based on what you described. The reason is [reason]. I would rather be straight with you than sell you something that does not match the goal.

## Spouse/Partner Follow-Up Close

Use when a real decision partner is involved.

Script:

> I respect that. Before we pause, are you personally clear that HTK is the right fit if your spouse is good with it?

If yes:

> Good. When will you speak with them? I will follow up at [specific time/date].

If no:

> Then let's clarify what is still unresolved before you bring it to them.

## Follow-Up Close

Use when a same-day decision is not appropriate but prospect remains qualified.

Script:

> I do not want to chase this indefinitely. The clean next step is we set one specific follow-up. If the answer is yes then, we move. If not, we close it out. Does [day/time] work?

## Closing Checklist

Before taking payment, confirm:

- Prospect fits HTK market.
- Prospect has a performance goal.
- Prospect has a meaningful obstacle.
- Prospect understands HTK is not bodybuilding-only.
- Prospect understands HTK is coaching, not medical care.
- Prospect is willing to train consistently.
- Prospect accepts accountability.
- Prospect understands current price and terms.
- Prospect is making a clear decision.
- Prospect is not under pressure or misunderstanding the offer.

## Stop Closing When

- Prospect says no.
- Prospect is not a fit.
- Prospect asks for medical/injury advice.
- Prospect wants a guarantee.
- Prospect becomes hostile.
- Prospect cannot afford the investment without real financial distress.
- Prospect will not commit to doing the work.
- Prospect only wants free coaching.
- Prospect needs a manager-level decision.
