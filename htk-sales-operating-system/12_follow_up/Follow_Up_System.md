# 12. Follow-Up System

Follow-up exists to help qualified prospects make a clear decision. It is not chasing, begging, or pressuring.

## Follow-Up Rules

- Follow up only when CRM shows the next action is due.
- Every follow-up must reference the actual call context.
- Do not restart dead follow-ups without new prospect engagement or manager approval.
- Do not follow up after opt-out.
- Do not keep vague prospects alive indefinitely.
- Stop when the prospect gives a clear no, becomes unsafe, or is no longer fit.

## Follow-Up Cadence

| Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Clarify next step while call is fresh |
| Day 3 | Re-anchor goal and unresolved decision |
| Day 7 | Decision check |
| Day 14 | Respectful close-loop |
| Day 30 | Relationship-based reactivation only if appropriate |

## Day 1 Follow-Up

Use when prospect left the call qualified but undecided.

Framework:

- Reference the call.
- Reference goal.
- Ask what remains unresolved.

Example:

> Good talking today. You said the main goal is getting your conditioning and athleticism back, and the gap is structure and consistency. What is the main thing you still need to sort through before deciding on HTK?

If spouse/partner:

> Good talking today. You were going to talk with your spouse about the investment and schedule. Are you still on track to have that conversation today?

## Day 3 Follow-Up

Use when Day 1 did not produce a decision.

Framework:

- Re-anchor cost of inaction.
- Ask for clear direction.

Example:

> Circling back. You said staying where you are means continuing to feel slow, stiff, and inconsistent. Do you want to move forward with HTK, or should I close this out for now?

## Day 7 Follow-Up

Use as a decision check.

Framework:

- Respect the prospect's time.
- Ask for yes/no direction.

Example:

> I do not want to keep this open without a clear next step. Is HTK something you want to start, or is the timing not right?

## Day 14 Follow-Up

Use to close the loop.

Framework:

- Close respectfully.
- Leave door open only if appropriate.

Example:

> I am going to close the loop for now. If building strength, conditioning, mobility, and athleticism becomes a priority again, message us and we can revisit whether HTK is a fit.

CRM action:

- Move to Closed Lost.
- Reason: Timing, No decision, or Not interested as appropriate.

## Day 30 Follow-Up

Use only when:

- Prospect was qualified.
- Prospect did not opt out.
- Prospect was respectful.
- There is a real reason to re-engage.

Example:

> Checking back once. Last time we talked, the goal was getting your conditioning and athleticism back, but timing was the issue. Is that still something you want to solve, or should we leave it closed?

## No-Show Follow-Up

After missed call:

> Looks like the call did not connect today. If you still want to talk through HTK, send two windows that work over the next seven days and we can reset it.

Final no-show follow-up after three calendar days:

> Closing the loop on the missed call. If you want to revisit HTK later, reach back out and we will pick it up from there.

CRM action:

- Closed Lost reason: No-show no reschedule.

## Follow-Up Conversion Tracking

Track:

- Follow-ups sent
- Replies from follow-up
- Calls rescheduled from follow-up
- Enrollments from follow-up
- Revenue from follow-up
- Closed lost after follow-up
