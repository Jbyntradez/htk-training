# 8. Objection Handling Library

HTK objection handling is not argument handling. The closer clarifies what the objection means, checks fit, and helps the prospect make a clear decision.

## Universal Objection Framework

1. Pause.
2. Acknowledge without agreeing to a false premise.
3. Clarify what the objection really means.
4. Tie back to the prospect's goal and consequence.
5. Decide whether to continue, follow up, or stop.

Use:

> That makes sense. When you say that, is the concern more about [fit/timing/investment/commitment/decision process]?

## "I Need To Think About It."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- I am not clear.
- I am afraid to decide.
- I do not see enough value.
- I have an unspoken objection.
- I am avoiding a no.

### Questions To Ask

- "What part do you need to think through?"
- "Is it the fit, the investment, the timing, or whether you will actually follow through?"
- "What information would help you make a clear decision?"
- "If you did think about it and nothing changed, what would you be deciding later that you cannot decide now?"

### Appropriate Response

> I am good with you making a clear decision. I do not want you saying yes if it is not right. But I also do not want to leave this vague. What specifically feels unresolved?

If clear but nervous:

> That is normal. The question is whether the goal matters enough to commit to the structure now.

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when the prospect cannot name a real unresolved issue, refuses clarity, or wants indefinite delay. Schedule one specific follow-up or close as no decision.

## "It's Too Expensive."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- I do not see the value.
- I cannot afford it.
- I am comparing it to cheaper fitness options.
- I am not committed enough.
- I need payment clarity.

### Questions To Ask

- "Too expensive compared to what?"
- "Is the issue affordability, or is it whether you believe the coaching is worth it?"
- "If the investment were not the issue, would HTK be the right fit?"
- "What is it costing you to stay where you are?"
- "Would investing at this level create real financial stress?"

### Appropriate Response

> I hear you. HTK is not the cheapest option, and it is not meant to be. The question is whether the structure, coaching, and accountability solve the problem you told me you want solved.

If true financial distress:

> If this creates financial pressure, I do not want you forcing it. It is better to wait than enroll in a way that creates stress.

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when the prospect says the investment creates real financial distress, refuses to see value, or only wants the cheapest option.

## "I Don't Have Time."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- I do not believe I can execute.
- My schedule is chaotic.
- I am not prioritizing the goal.
- I need a plan that fits real life.

### Questions To Ask

- "Is time the real issue, or is the issue not having structure around the time you do have?"
- "How many days per week can you realistically train?"
- "What happens if your schedule stays the same and nothing changes?"
- "Would a structured plan make training easier to execute, or are you not ready to prioritize it?"

### Appropriate Response

> A busy schedule is exactly why structure matters. But HTK still requires you to train. If you can make time and need a plan around it, this can fit. If you are not willing to make time at all, it is not the right moment.

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when the prospect admits he will not make time or wants results without changing behavior.

## "I Can Do This Myself."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- I want control.
- I do not value coaching yet.
- I have tried solo but do not want to admit the gap.
- I may be right if I am already progressing.

### Questions To Ask

- "You might be able to. What has stopped you from doing it so far?"
- "What have you tried already?"
- "If doing it yourself was working, would we be having this conversation?"
- "What is the biggest thing you are missing: plan, accountability, progression, or consistency?"

### Appropriate Response

> If what you are doing is working, stay with it. HTK is for the guy who is willing to work but needs the right structure and accountability to get where he says he wants to go.

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when the prospect is progressing well alone, does not value coaching, or wants to prove independence more than solve the problem.

## "Maybe Later."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- Timing is unclear.
- Urgency is low.
- Fear is present.
- The goal may not matter enough yet.

### Questions To Ask

- "What would need to change later?"
- "Is later tied to a real date or just a way to avoid deciding?"
- "What happens if you wait 30, 60, or 90 days?"
- "Do you want me to follow up at a specific time, or should we leave it here?"

### Appropriate Response

> That may be the right answer if timing is truly off. I just want to separate real timing from avoiding the decision.

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when there is no real date, no urgency, or no willingness to define a next step.

## "I Need To Talk To My Spouse."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- There is a real decision partner.
- He is avoiding ownership.
- Money requires discussion.
- Spouse may have influence over schedule or budget.

### Questions To Ask

- "That makes sense. What do you need to talk through with them?"
- "Is your spouse deciding fit, budget, schedule, or support?"
- "If they are good with it, do you want to move forward?"
- "Would it make sense to bring them into the conversation?"
- "When will you speak with them?"

### Appropriate Response

> I respect that. Let's make the follow-up specific so this does not turn into a vague delay. When will you talk with them, and when should I follow up?

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when the prospect refuses to define the decision process or uses spouse as a blanket avoidance tactic.

## "I Want To Try Free Content First."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- I am not ready to invest.
- I do not understand coaching value.
- I want information, not accountability.
- I want proof without commitment.

### Questions To Ask

- "What are you hoping free content will solve?"
- "Have you already had access to enough information?"
- "Is the issue not knowing what to do, or not executing consistently?"
- "If free content does not change the result, what will you do next?"

### Appropriate Response

> Free content can help with ideas. Coaching is different. HTK is for structure, progression, accountability, and adjustment around your actual goal. If you only want ideas right now, free content is probably the better next step.

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when the prospect wants content instead of coaching or wants to delay without a decision date.

## "I'm Not Sure It'll Work For Me."

### What The Prospect Is Really Saying

- Fear of failure.
- Past programs did not work.
- Does not trust himself.
- Does not understand the coaching mechanism.
- Wants a guarantee.

### Questions To Ask

- "What makes you unsure?"
- "Is that based on HTK, or based on past attempts?"
- "What did not work before?"
- "Was the issue the plan, the accountability, the consistency, or the execution?"
- "If you had the right structure and actually followed it, do you believe progress is possible?"

### Appropriate Response

> I cannot guarantee an outcome, and I will not pretend to. What I can say is that the process gives you structure, coaching, and accountability around the goal. The part you control is execution.

### When To Stop Pushing

Stop when the prospect requires a guarantee, refuses responsibility, or is not willing to execute.
