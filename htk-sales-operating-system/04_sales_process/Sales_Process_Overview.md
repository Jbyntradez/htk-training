# 4. Sales Process Overview

The HTK sales process must be stage-based, measurable, and repeatable.

## Pipeline

Lead -> Qualified -> Discovery Call -> Offer Presentation -> Decision -> Client Onboarding

## Stage Definitions

### 1. Lead

Definition: A prospect exists in the CRM and has shown enough context to be considered for sales follow-up.

Required fields:

- Name
- Contact info
- Lead source
- Segment
- Initial goal or reason for interest
- Current stage
- Next action
- Next-action date

Move forward when:

- Prospect meets basic target-market fit and agrees to a call or is assigned to closer as call-ready.

Move out when:

- Prospect is duplicate, underage, opt-out, spam, poor fit, or unresponsive after approved follow-up.

### 2. Qualified

Definition: A prospect has enough pre-call information to justify a discovery call.

Minimum qualification:

- Fits HTK market.
- Has a performance-related goal or likely performance need.
- Has a meaningful obstacle or reason to change.
- Is open to coaching.
- Can attend a call within the scheduled window.

Move forward when:

- Call is booked and confirmed.

### 3. Discovery Call

Definition: A live conversation where the closer diagnoses fit, goal, obstacle, urgency, commitment, and decision ability.

Closer must confirm:

- Performance goal
- Athletic/training background
- Current situation
- Main limitations
- Consequences of staying the same
- Desired outcome
- Commitment level
- Decision process
- Red flags

Move forward when:

- Prospect is fit, committed, and has a reason to consider HTK now.

Move out when:

- Prospect is not fit, not committed, unsafe, under 18, or seeking a service HTK does not provide.

### 4. Offer Presentation

Definition: The closer explains HTK coaching as the solution only after discovery confirms fit.

Required presentation points:

- Why HTK fits the prospect's stated goal.
- What the coaching process does.
- What accountability looks like.
- What the client is expected to do.
- What decision the prospect needs to make.
- Current approved price, terms, and enrollment method.

Move forward when:

- Prospect clearly understands the offer and gives a decision.

### 5. Decision

Definition: Prospect chooses one of the approved outcomes.

Approved decision outcomes:

- Enrolled
- Follow-up scheduled
- Closed Lost - Not interested
- Closed Lost - Not a fit
- Closed Lost - Timing
- Closed Lost - Financial
- Closed Lost - No decision
- Escalated to manager

Move forward when:

- Payment is completed and onboarding handoff begins.

### 6. Client Onboarding

Definition: New client is handed from sales into the HTK onboarding process.

Required completion:

- Payment complete
- Enrollment confirmation sent
- Onboarding instructions sent
- CRM updated to Closed Won
- Handoff note submitted
- Any constraints or relevant context documented

## Pipeline Rules

- A prospect cannot enter Offer Presentation before the closer completes discovery.
- A prospect cannot be marked Closed Won until payment is confirmed.
- A prospect cannot be marked Client Onboarding until the handoff is complete.
- A prospect cannot be left without a next action unless the stage is final.
- Poor-fit prospects must be closed cleanly instead of kept alive indefinitely.
