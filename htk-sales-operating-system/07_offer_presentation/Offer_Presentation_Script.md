# 7. Offer Presentation Script

The offer presentation happens only after discovery confirms fit.

## Presentation Standard

The HTK offer must sound:

- Clear
- Calm
- Professional
- Performance-driven
- Specific to the prospect's goal

The HTK offer must not sound:

- Hype-driven
- Desperate
- Manipulative
- Like a guarantee
- Like bodybuilding-only coaching
- Like medical treatment

## Transition Into Offer

Use:

> Based on what you told me, the gap is not effort. You have been willing to train. The gap is structure, direction, and accountability around the kind of performance you want to build. HTK is designed for that. I will walk you through how it works, then we can decide whether it is the right fit.

## Full HTK Offer Presentation

Use this structure. Adapt the details to the prospect's stated goal and current approved offer terms.

### 1. Re-anchor The Prospect's Goal

> What I heard is that you want to [prospect's goal]. The big thing holding it back is [obstacle], and if nothing changes you are likely going to keep running into [consequence].

Confirm:

> Is that accurate?

### 2. Position HTK

> HTK is performance coaching for men who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, athletic, and capable in real life. It is not bodybuilding-only coaching. The work is built around strength, conditioning, mobility, durability, and athleticism so your training carries over outside the gym.

### 3. Explain The Coaching Process

> The coaching process starts by understanding where you are now and what you are trying to build. From there, the coach gives you structure around the goal: what to train, how to progress, how to balance strength and conditioning, and how to stay consistent around your actual schedule.

Core process:

- Goal and baseline review
- Training direction based on performance outcome
- Structured programming
- Conditioning and mobility integrated with strength
- Accountability and communication
- Progress review and adjustment

### 4. Explain Accountability

> The accountability is not just someone cheering you on. It is having a standard, a plan, and a coach who can see whether you are executing. If something is not working, the answer is not to disappear or guess. The answer is to communicate, adjust, and keep moving.

What accountability means:

- Follow the assigned plan.
- Communicate with the coach.
- Report issues early.
- Track required training markers.
- Stay honest about consistency.
- Adjust when schedule, recovery, or performance demands change.

### 5. Explain Expected Commitment

> HTK works when the client does the work. You do not need a perfect schedule, but you do need to be willing to train consistently, communicate, and follow the structure. If you are not ready for that, I would rather tell you now.

Expected client commitment:

- Train consistently.
- Follow the coaching plan.
- Communicate honestly.
- Ask questions before improvising.
- Take ownership of schedule and effort.
- Stay engaged long enough for the process to work.

### 6. Explain Fit

> The reason I think this can fit you is [specific reason from discovery]. You are trying to [goal], and the current gap is [obstacle]. HTK is built for that kind of performance problem.

If not fit:

> I do not think HTK is the cleanest fit for what you described.

### 7. Present Current Terms

Use only current manager-approved pricing, term length, payment options, onboarding details, and refund policy.

Script:

> The current coaching option is [approved offer]. It includes [approved deliverables]. The investment is [approved price/terms]. If you enroll today, the next step is payment, then we hand you into onboarding so the coaching team can get the intake completed and start building from there.

Do not invent:

- Discounts
- Bonuses
- Scarcity
- Guarantees
- Refund terms
- Coach access
- Timeline promises

### 8. Check Understanding

Ask:

- "What questions do you have about how the coaching works?"
- "Does the structure match what you said you need?"
- "Is there anything that feels unclear?"

### 9. Move To Decision

Use:

> Based on everything we covered, do you feel like HTK is the right fit for what you are trying to build?

If yes:

> Good. Then the next step is getting you enrolled and handed into onboarding.

If unsure:

> What part are you unsure about: the fit, the commitment, the timing, or the investment?

## Offer Presentation Rules

- Do not present before discovery.
- Tie the offer to the prospect's words.
- Never guarantee a result.
- Do not pressure a poor-fit prospect.
- Do not over-explain.
- Do not hide the commitment required.
- Do not handle medical questions beyond referral to qualified healthcare professionals.
- Do not proceed if the prospect does not understand what they are buying.
