# 10. Payment & Enrollment Process

The enrollment process turns a clear decision into a clean client handoff. The closer must not improvise payment terms or onboarding promises.

## Enrollment Checklist

Before payment:

- [ ] Prospect is qualified.
- [ ] Offer was presented after discovery.
- [ ] Prospect gave a clear yes.
- [ ] Current approved price and terms were stated accurately.
- [ ] Prospect understands HTK is performance coaching.
- [ ] Prospect understands no results are guaranteed.
- [ ] Prospect understands expected commitment.
- [ ] Any injury/medical context is documented without advice.
- [ ] CRM notes are complete enough for onboarding.

## Payment Workflow

Use only the manager-approved payment method.

1. Confirm the prospect's legal name and best email.
2. Confirm selected approved offer/payment option.
3. Send the approved payment link or invoice.
4. Stay available while the prospect completes payment.
5. Confirm payment status in the approved system.
6. Do not mark Closed Won until payment is confirmed.
7. Update CRM stage to `Closed Won`.
8. Record payment date, offer, price/terms, and payment confirmation reference if applicable.
9. Send enrollment confirmation message.
10. Submit onboarding handoff.

## Payment Language

Use:

> Good. The next step is payment and enrollment. I am sending the approved payment link now. Once that is completed, I will confirm it on my end and hand you into onboarding.

After payment:

> You are enrolled. I have payment confirmed, and I am handing you into onboarding now. Watch for the next steps at the email you provided.

If payment fails:

> It looks like the payment did not go through. Check the payment details and try once more. If it still does not work, I will flag it for support.

## Do Not

- Do not collect card details manually.
- Do not ask for passwords or sensitive financial information.
- Do not process payment through unapproved tools.
- Do not promise discounts, refunds, extensions, or bonuses without approval.
- Do not mark Closed Won before payment confirmation.
- Do not enroll a prospect who changes his mind during payment.
- Do not pressure a prospect through a payment issue.

## Next Steps After Payment

Immediately after payment confirmation:

1. Send confirmation.
2. Update CRM.
3. Submit handoff to onboarding.
4. Record any scheduling, injury, equipment, or lifestyle context the coach needs.
5. Confirm the client knows where onboarding instructions will arrive.
6. Notify manager if payment, expectation, or onboarding issue appears.

## Handoff To Onboarding

The handoff must include:

- Client name
- Email
- Phone if approved and collected
- Lead source
- Segment
- Goal
- Main obstacle
- Why now
- Training background
- Lifestyle constraints
- Injury considerations or clearance notes
- Commitment notes
- Offer purchased
- Payment confirmation
- Any risk notes

## Enrollment Quality Standard

A clean enrollment means:

- Client knows what he bought.
- Client knows what happens next.
- CRM is complete.
- Onboarding has the context needed to start.
- No unapproved promises were made.
