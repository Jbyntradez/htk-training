# Prospecting SOP

## Objective

Build a daily list of relevant men who are likely to value athletic, durable, real-world performance.

The VA researches manually. The VA does not scrape profiles, buy lead lists, use automated search tools, or send messages from a bulk sender.

## Daily Prospecting Target

Research enough prospects to send 20 high-quality first-contact DMs.

Recommended daily research:

| Activity | Target |
| --- | ---: |
| Profiles reviewed | 35-50 |
| New leads logged | 22-30 |
| First-contact DMs sent | 20 |

Expect to reject profiles. A smaller clean list is better than a large weak list.

## Ideal Prospect Signals

Prioritize profiles with at least two signals:

- Mentions a sport, athletic background, military service, veteran status, first-responder role, or performance training.
- Posts about strength, conditioning, running, mobility, recovery, durability, training consistency, or returning to athletic form.
- Follows or interacts with performance coaches, sports accounts, tactical fitness accounts, or training discussions.
- Expresses frustration with feeling slow, stiff, out of shape, inconsistent, or less athletic than before.
- Posts about preparing for a physical goal such as a season, tryout, race, fitness test, return to sport, or demanding work.
- Appears to be a man age 18-45 based on public profile context.

Do not guess at private traits. Use only public context.

## Priority Segments

| Priority | Segment | Common signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Athlete | Current sport, competition, team, season, tryout, measurable performance goal |
| 1 | Former athlete | Mentions prior sport, getting athletic again, stiffness, conditioning loss, or return to training |
| 1 | Military or veteran | Publicly states service and discusses fitness, readiness, durability, or training |
| 1 | First responder | Publicly states role and discusses training, readiness, or physical demands |
| 2 | Performance-focused fitness enthusiast | Trains consistently and values strength, conditioning, mobility, or performance |
| 2 | High performer | Wants a structured way to stay capable despite demanding work and schedule |

## Do Not Contact

Do not contact:

- Anyone who appears to be under 18.
- Anyone who has asked HTK not to contact them.
- Anyone already marked `Closed Lost` due to opt-out or rejection.
- Anyone with no credible fit to the HTK market.
- Private accounts with no public context that supports a relevant message.
- Obvious spam, bot, fake, or impersonation accounts.
- Accounts centered on explicit content, hate, harassment, or illegal activity.
- Prospects whose only signal is a trending topic or popular hashtag.
- Current HTK members unless the purpose is customer support and the manager assigns it.

## Manual Research Sources On X

Use these sources in order:

1. People who meaningfully interact with HTK posts.
2. Followers and following lists of relevant performance accounts.
3. Replies to useful discussions about athletic training, conditioning, mobility, durability, and returning to sport.
4. Manual X search for relevant phrases such as:
   - `former athlete training`
   - `getting athletic again`
   - `need better conditioning`
   - `training for fitness test`
   - `mobility work`
   - `return to sport`
   - `tactical fitness`
5. Relevant public profile bios discovered naturally during research.

Search is for discovery only. Do not message every person in search results.

## Prospect Fit Score

Score every prospect before sending a DM:

| Criterion | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Clear target-market identity | 0-2 |
| Public interest in performance training | 0-2 |
| Current training goal or frustration | 0-2 |
| Recent activity within 30 days | 0-1 |
| Natural conversation opener exists | 0-1 |

Decision:

| Score | Action |
| ---: | --- |
| 6-8 | Prioritize for outreach |
| 4-5 | Contact only if the opener is specific and relevant |
| 0-3 | Do not contact |

## Research And Contact Workflow

For each prospect:

1. Open the profile.
2. Read the bio.
3. Review at least five recent posts or replies when available.
4. Identify the segment.
5. Identify one real public signal.
6. Search the CRM by X handle and profile URL.
7. Skip the prospect if a record already exists unless a follow-up is due.
8. Calculate the fit score.
9. Log prospects scoring 4 or higher as `New Lead`.
10. Draft a contextual first-contact DM.
11. Send only if the message sounds natural and relevant.
12. Move the CRM record to `Contacted`.
13. Set Follow-Up 1 for three calendar days later.

## Personalization Note Standard

The CRM public-signal note must be specific enough that another team member understands why the VA contacted the prospect.

Good notes:

- `Former college safety. Posted May 31 that he wants his first-step speed back after years of strength-only training.`
- `Veteran. Bio mentions tactical fitness. Recent post says conditioning has dropped since switching to a desk role.`
- `Runs and lifts consistently. Asked a mobility coach how to stop feeling stiff after heavy lower-body days.`

Bad notes:

- `Likes fitness`
- `Good lead`
- `Athlete`

## Quality Check Before Sending

Ask:

1. Does this person fit HTK?
2. Can I explain why I am contacting him?
3. Is the message easy to answer?
4. Does it sound like a human wrote it after reading the profile?
5. Would this message still make sense if HTK never sold anything?

If any answer is no, do not send the message.
