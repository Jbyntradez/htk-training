# Follow-Up Scripts

## Follow-Up Philosophy

Follow-up is a reminder, not pressure.

The VA sends a maximum of three follow-ups after a first-contact DM. Stop sooner when the prospect replies, declines, or asks not to receive messages.

Do not send additional follow-ups after the final message. Do not restart a closed sequence without manager approval or a new prospect-initiated interaction.

## Follow-Up Schedule

| Step | Timing | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Follow-Up 1 | 3 calendar days after first contact | Light bump |
| Follow-Up 2 | 5 calendar days after Follow-Up 1 | Add a useful either-or question |
| Follow-Up 3 | 7 calendar days after Follow-Up 2 | Close the loop respectfully |

If the prospect replies at any point, stop the sequence and continue the live conversation.

## No-Reply Follow-Up Scripts

### Follow-Up 1: Simple Bump

> Wanted to bring this back up in case it got buried. What are you focused on most in your training right now?

### Follow-Up 1: Former Athlete

> Circling back because the former-athlete side of your profile stood out. Are you still training for performance, or mainly trying to get consistency back?

### Follow-Up 1: Conditioning

> Bringing this back up because conditioning is usually the first thing that gets pushed aside. Are you actively working on it right now?

### Follow-Up 2: Priority Choice

> Quick one: if you could improve one thing over the next few months, would it be strength, conditioning, mobility, speed, or feeling athletic again?

### Follow-Up 2: Current Plan

> Are you following a structured plan right now, or piecing training together week to week?

### Follow-Up 3: Close The Loop

> I will leave it there after this. If building more strength, conditioning, mobility, and athleticism becomes a priority, feel free to reach out.

## Conversation Follow-Ups

Use these only when a prospect replied previously and then went quiet.

### After Goal Discussion

Send after three calendar days:

> You mentioned wanting to improve your conditioning. Is that still something you want to solve now?

### After Obstacle Discussion

Send after three calendar days:

> You mentioned consistency has been the issue when work gets busy. Would a structured plan help, or is the timing not right?

### After Call Invitation

Send after two calendar days:

> Still want me to set up a conversation with Jimmy?

### After Availability Request

Send after two calendar days:

> I can still get the call set up. Send your time zone, best calendar email, and two windows that work over the next week.

### Final Booking Close-Out

Send five calendar days after the availability request:

> I am going to close the loop for now. If you want to revisit the call later, message us and we will pick it back up.

## Follow-Up Decision Rules

| Situation | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Prospect says no | Stop immediately and mark `Closed Lost` |
| Prospect asks to stop | Stop immediately, mark `Closed Lost`, and record `Opt-out` |
| Prospect does not reply after Follow-Up 3 | Mark `Closed Lost` with reason `No response after final follow-up` |
| Prospect replies but timing is later | Ask whether to leave it there; respect the answer |
| Prospect becomes hostile | Stop, record the reason, and escalate if needed |
| X displays a warning or DM limitation | Stop outbound messages and alert the manager |
