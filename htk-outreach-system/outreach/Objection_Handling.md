# Objection Handling

## Rule

The VA does not argue, pressure, discount, or attempt to close. The VA answers briefly and decides whether to continue, invite a call, or close the conversation respectfully.

## Approved Responses

### "What are you selling?"

> Fair question. HTK is a monthly performance coaching membership built around strength, conditioning, mobility, athleticism, and durability. I am not here to hard-sell you. I was trying to understand whether it is even relevant to what you are training for.

### "How much is it?"

> Jimmy covers the coaching structure and current membership details on the call because the first step is making sure HTK actually fits your goal. If you are open to that, I can set up a short conversation.

If the prospect asks again:

> I do not want to give you incomplete information in DMs. If a call is not something you want, no problem at all.

### "Just send me the link."

> Happy to get you the right next step. Before I do, what are you trying to improve most right now?

If the prospect refuses to answer:

> Understood. HTK is coaching for athleticism, strength, conditioning, mobility, and durability. If you decide you want to talk through whether it fits, message us and I will help set up a call.

### "I already have a program."

> Good. If it is moving you toward the goal, stay with it. Is there anything you still feel is missing?

If the prospect says no:

> Sounds like you are covered. Keep getting after it.

### "I do not have time."

> Understood. Is the schedule the reason you are not looking at coaching right now, or is fitting training into the week the problem you are trying to solve?

If timing is wrong:

> Got it. I will leave it there.

### "I am not interested."

> Understood. I will leave it there. Appreciate the reply.

Mark `Closed Lost` and do not follow up.

### "Stop messaging me."

> Understood. You will not receive another message from us.

Mark `Closed Lost`, record `Opt-out`, and do not contact again.

### "Is this automated?"

> No. I am part of the HTK team and I am messaging manually after reviewing profiles that look relevant to performance training. If you would rather not hear from us again, I will leave it there.

### "Can you make me a workout?"

> I cannot prescribe training through DMs. If you want coaching built around your goals, I can help set up a conversation with Jimmy.

### "I have an injury. What should I do?"

> I cannot give medical advice. Please speak with a qualified healthcare professional. I am going to flag your message for the team before discussing any training next step.

Escalate immediately.

### "Can you guarantee results?"

> No. Results depend on the individual, the plan, and the work. The call is for understanding your goal and deciding whether the coaching is a fit.

### "I only want to get bigger."

> HTK is built around athletic performance, durability, strength, conditioning, and mobility. If appearance-only bodybuilding is the priority, HTK may not be the right fit.

### "Maybe later."

> Understood. Want me to leave it there for now?

If yes, mark `Closed Lost` with reason `Timing - prospect asked to pause`.

### "Who is Jimmy?"

> Jimmy Bynum is the founder. His background includes multi-sport athletics, college football at Northern Michigan, and the Marine Corps. He built HTK around training that carries over outside the gym.

### "Why did you message me?"

Give the real reason. Example:

> Your post about wanting your conditioning back after years of strength-focused training looked relevant to what HTK works on. I wanted to ask where you were trying to take it.

Never invent a reason.

## When To End The Conversation

End the conversation when:

- The prospect declines.
- The prospect asks to stop.
- The prospect is not an HTK fit.
- The prospect becomes hostile.
- The conversation requires medical, legal, or customer-service handling.
- The prospect wants a detailed sales discussion in DMs that should happen with Jimmy.
