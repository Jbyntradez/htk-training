# Outreach Scripts

## How To Use These Scripts

These are approved starting points. They are not bulk-send templates.

Before sending:

1. Read the prospect's bio.
2. Review recent posts.
3. Choose the script that matches a real signal.
4. Adapt the first sentence when a specific detail makes the message more natural.
5. Ask only one question.
6. Do not send the same exact DM repeatedly.

Keep first-contact DMs short. Do not add a booking link. Do not pitch the membership in the first message.

## First-Contact Scripts

### Former Athlete: Training Style

> You still training with an athletic focus, or has it turned into mostly strength work these days?

### Former Athlete: Wants Athleticism Back

> Saw your post about wanting to feel athletic again. What feels furthest off right now: conditioning, mobility, or explosiveness?

### Athlete: Current Goal

> Looks like you are still putting real work in. What are you training for right now?

### Athlete: Speed Or Explosiveness

> Your post about getting faster caught my attention. Are you focused more on first-step explosiveness or overall conditioning right now?

### Military Or Veteran: Durability

> Saw the service background in your profile. Are you training more for strength these days, or trying to keep conditioning and durability in the mix too?

### First Responder: Readiness

> Respect the work you do. Are you training around job readiness right now, or mainly trying to stay consistent around the schedule?

### Conditioning Frustration

> Saw your point about strength being there but conditioning falling off. What kind of conditioning are you trying to get back to?

### Mobility Frustration

> Your comment about feeling stiff after lifting was real. Is mobility the main thing limiting your training right now?

### Strength-Only Plateau

> You have a solid strength base. Are you looking to keep pushing numbers, or build more speed and conditioning around it?

### Busy High Performer

> Looks like you have a full schedule and still make training a priority. What is harder to keep dialed in right now: consistency, conditioning, or recovery?

### Running And Strength

> You are doing both running and lifting. Are you training for a specific event, or trying to build a better all-around engine?

### Return To Training

> Saw that you are getting back into training. What are you trying to rebuild first?

### Existing HTK Post Engagement

> Appreciate you jumping into that HTK post. What are you working on most right now in your own training?

## Response Scripts

### Prospect Gives A Goal

> Got it. What has been the main thing holding that back?

### Prospect Gives A Vague Answer

> Makes sense. If you had to pick one priority for the next few months, would it be strength, conditioning, mobility, speed, or just feeling athletic again?

### Prospect Says He Is Inconsistent

> Understood. Is the issue mostly time, not having a clear plan, or falling off when the week gets busy?

### Prospect Says He Feels Stiff

> That is common when training gets strength-heavy. Is the stiffness affecting performance, or mainly how you feel day to day?

### Prospect Says Conditioning Is The Problem

> What would better conditioning let you do that you cannot do comfortably right now?

### Prospect Says He Wants To Be Athletic Again

> That is exactly the kind of goal HTK is built around. What do you miss most: moving fast, lasting longer, or feeling capable across the board?

### Prospect Has A Clear Goal And Obstacle

> Makes sense. Is that something you want to fix now, or are you mostly exploring ideas for later?

### Prospect Wants To Act Now

> Would you be open to a structured coaching plan if it matched what you are trying to build?

## HTK Explanation Scripts

### What Is HTK?

> HTK is performance coaching for men who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, and capable in real life. It is built around athleticism and durability, not bodybuilding-only training.

### Who Is Jimmy?

> Jimmy Bynum is the founder. His background includes multi-sport athletics, college football at Northern Michigan, and the Marine Corps. He built HTK around training that carries over outside the gym.

### Is This A Program?

> HTK is a monthly coaching membership. The right next step is a short conversation with Jimmy so he can understand the goal and see whether the coaching is a fit.

## Booking Transition

Use after the prospect meets the qualification gate:

> Based on what you said, it sounds like a conversation with Jimmy would be useful. He can look at where you are now, what you are training for, and whether HTK is a fit. No pressure either way. Want me to set that up?

## Tone Check

Before sending any message, remove:

- Hype
- Long explanations
- Multiple questions
- Forced compliments
- Unnecessary links
- Any promise of results
- Any claim that Jimmy personally reviewed the prospect unless he actually did
