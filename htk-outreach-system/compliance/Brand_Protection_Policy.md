# Brand Protection Policy

## Purpose

Every VA message represents Hard To Kill Training and Jimmy Bynum. Protect the brand before chasing a metric.

## Brand Standard

HTK sounds:

- Direct
- Masculine
- Disciplined
- Professional
- Human
- Performance-driven

HTK does not sound:

- Corporate
- Hype-driven
- Desperate
- Aggressive
- Fake
- Condescending
- Overly familiar

## Approved Claims

The VA may say:

> HTK is performance coaching for men who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, and capable in real life. It is built around athleticism and durability, not bodybuilding-only training.

The VA may say:

> Jimmy Bynum is the founder. His background includes multi-sport athletics, college football at Northern Michigan, and the Marine Corps. He built HTK around training that carries over outside the gym.

The VA may say:

> HTK is a monthly coaching membership.

## Prohibited Claims

Do not say:

- HTK guarantees results.
- HTK will prevent injury.
- HTK will cure pain or fix a medical condition.
- Jimmy personally reviewed the prospect unless he actually did.
- A spot is about to disappear unless the manager confirms a real capacity limit.
- The prospect was selected for an exclusive offer unless that is true and approved.
- HTK is the best, fastest, only, or guaranteed solution.

## Tone Rules

- Keep messages short.
- Ask one question at a time.
- Use the prospect's words when describing the goal.
- Respect a no immediately.
- Do not shame someone for being out of shape, inconsistent, stiff, or slower than before.
- Do not use profanity from the HTK account.
- Do not use forced military language.
- Do not overuse exclamation marks.
- Do not debate politics, religion, or controversial topics.

## Public Interaction Rules

- Do not argue in public.
- Do not share DM content publicly.
- Do not mock prospects or competitors.
- Do not comment on a prospect's body.
- Do not post or reply with medical advice.
- Do not respond to harassment beyond a neutral close.

Approved close:

> Understood. We will leave it there.

## Escalation Rules

Stop and escalate:

- Medical or injury questions
- Customer complaints
- Refund or charge issues
- Partnership requests
- Sponsorship requests
- Media requests
- Public figures, professional athletes, large creators, and organization accounts
- Legal threats
- Harassment, threats, or explicit content
- Any message you are unsure how to handle

## Information Handling

- Store only the minimum operational information.
- Never publish DM content.
- Never share prospect details outside authorized HTK systems.
- Never store credentials.
- Use calendar emails only for scheduling and related reminders.
- Keep CRM notes factual and respectful.

## Brand-Risk Incident Workflow

1. Stop replying.
2. Take a screenshot if appropriate.
3. Record the lead handle and a factual summary.
4. Notify the manager immediately.
5. Wait for an approved response.
6. Record the final action in the CRM.

## Final Test

Before sending, ask:

> Would Jimmy be comfortable seeing this message publicly with the HTK name attached?

If the answer is not clearly yes, do not send it.
