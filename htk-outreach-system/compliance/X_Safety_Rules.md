# X Safety Rules

## Purpose

Protect the HTK account, the prospect experience, and the company reputation.

This SOP is based on official X guidance reviewed on June 2, 2026. X policies can change. The manager must review the official links monthly.

## HTK Operating Rule

All HTK outreach is manual and contextual.

The VA must not use:

- Auto-DM tools
- Browser scripts
- Scrapers
- Bots
- Bulk senders
- Engagement automation
- Automated likes
- Automated follow or unfollow tools
- Automated list-building tools
- Multiple duplicate accounts
- Fake personas
- Tools designed to bypass X limits or enforcement

X's automation rules state that non-API website scripting can lead to permanent suspension. HTK does not use scripted website automation.

## Daily HTK Caps

| Activity | Target | HTK cap |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| New personalized DMs | 20 | 25 |
| Follow-ups | 15 | 20 |

X currently publishes a technical daily DM limit of 500 messages. That is not an HTK goal or permission to send aggressive outreach. HTK uses much lower internal caps to prioritize relevant conversations.

## Messaging Rules

- Research before messaging.
- Message only prospects with a credible HTK fit.
- Use a real public signal.
- Ask one easy-to-answer question.
- Do not send identical DMs repeatedly.
- Do not send bulk, aggressive, or high-volume unsolicited messages.
- Do not send a link in the first-contact DM.
- Do not send repeated link-only messages.
- Do not message prospects only because they used a trending hashtag.
- Do not use misleading links.
- Stop immediately after an opt-out or rejection.
- Stop the no-reply sequence after the final follow-up.

## Account Rules

- Use only the authorized HTK account.
- Use only the approved access method.
- Do not create additional HTK outreach accounts.
- Do not share login credentials in the CRM, DMs, email, or chat.
- Do not approve third-party access without written manager approval.
- Do not attempt to bypass warnings, limitations, or enforcement.
- Do not delete and resend messages to conceal mistakes.

## Privacy Rules

- Use public information only for prospecting.
- Ask only for the minimum information needed to schedule a call.
- Collect calendar email, time zone, and availability only after the prospect agrees to schedule.
- Do not request passwords, payment information, government ID, home address, or medical details.
- Do not copy private DM information into public posts.
- Keep CRM notes concise, factual, and limited to operational needs.

## Stop-Work Triggers

Stop outbound messaging and alert the manager immediately when:

- X displays a warning, DM limitation, or account restriction.
- You suspect unauthorized login activity.
- A tool requests account credentials unexpectedly.
- Multiple prospects complain about outreach in a short period.
- You accidentally send private information.
- You accidentally send an unapproved link.
- You are instructed to use automation or bypass a limit.

Do not resume until the manager approves.

## Official Sources

- [X Rules](https://help.x.com/en/rules-and-policies/x-rules)
- [X automation development rules](https://help.x.com/en/rules-and-policies/x-automation)
- [X Direct Message FAQs](https://help.x.com/en/using-twitter/direct-message-faqs.html)
- [Understanding X limits](https://help.x.com/en/rules-and-policies/x-limits)

Key points from the official sources:

- X prohibits platform manipulation and spam.
- X warns against bulk, aggressive, high-volume unsolicited DMs.
- X warns that duplicate DMs to multiple accounts can be reported as spam.
- X prohibits unsolicited automated DMs.
- X prohibits non-API scripted website automation.
- X's technical account limits do not override rules against aggressive behavior.
