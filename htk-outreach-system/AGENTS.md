# AGENTS.md

## Mission

This repository governs manual X outreach for Hard To Kill Training (HTK). Any AI agent editing these files must preserve an operational system that a newly hired VA can execute within 24 hours.

Do not turn the repository into general marketing advice. Write exact steps, exact decision rules, exact scripts, and measurable management standards.

## Company Context

Hard To Kill Training is founded by Jimmy Bynum:

- Former multi-sport athlete
- Football scholarship athlete at Northern Michigan University
- Background in football, wrestling, basketball, and track
- United States Marine Corps background

HTK is a premium performance and fitness coaching brand. It is not bodybuilding coaching.

## Target Market

Primary audience: men ages 18-45.

Priority prospects:

1. Athletes
2. Former athletes
3. Active military and veterans
4. First responders
5. Fitness enthusiasts who want performance, not appearance-only training
6. High performers who value durability, mobility, endurance, and functional strength

## Brand Voice

Every script, SOP, report, and policy must sound:

- Direct
- Masculine
- Disciplined
- Professional
- Human
- Performance-driven

Avoid:

- Corporate filler
- Hype
- Aggressive sales language
- Bodybuilding-only language
- False urgency
- Guarantees
- Shame
- Fake familiarity
- Military cosplay or forced toughness

Use clear language. Speak like a capable human being who respects the prospect's time.

## Outreach Philosophy

The VA does not sell. The VA starts conversations, builds rapport, qualifies prospects, and schedules calls.

HTK outreach must remain manual, contextual, and respectful:

- Research before messaging.
- Personalize from a real public signal.
- Use scripts as frameworks, not as mass-send copy.
- Prefer a useful question over a pitch.
- Do not send a link in the first message.
- Ask only for the minimum information needed to qualify and schedule.
- Stop immediately after a rejection or opt-out.

Do not add scraping, bulk sending, browser scripting, auto-DM tools, fake accounts, engagement automation, or methods intended to bypass X restrictions.

## Lead Stages

The CRM stage list is fixed:

1. New Lead
2. Contacted
3. Responded
4. Engaged
5. Qualified
6. Call Scheduled
7. Closed Won
8. Closed Lost

Do not rename stages casually. Any new stage must solve a real reporting problem and must be reflected in every CRM and KPI document.

## Daily Standards

Steady-state daily targets:

| KPI | Target | Cap |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| New personalized DMs sent | 20 | 25 |
| Follow-ups sent | 15 | 20 |
| Conversations started | 6 | None |
| Qualified leads | 2 | None |
| Calls scheduled | 1 | None |

Quality outranks volume. Agents must not increase caps unless official X rules are reviewed and the manager explicitly approves the change.

## Qualification Standard

A lead becomes `Qualified` only when all of the following are confirmed:

- The prospect fits the HTK market.
- The prospect has a performance-related goal.
- The prospect has a meaningful obstacle or reason the goal matters now.
- The prospect is open to coaching support.
- The prospect is willing to speak with Jimmy within the next 14 days.

Do not qualify a lead merely because the prospect replied.

## Management Standard

Every operational update must preserve:

- Daily reporting
- Weekly QA review
- Message sampling
- CRM completeness checks
- Compliance review
- Call quality feedback
- Scorecard-based coaching for the VA

Do not judge a VA only by volume. Evaluate message quality, CRM accuracy, prospect fit, responsiveness, booked-call quality, and compliance.
