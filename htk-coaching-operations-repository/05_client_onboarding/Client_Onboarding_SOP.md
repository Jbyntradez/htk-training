# 5. Client Onboarding SOP

## Objective

Move a paid client from enrollment into active coaching with clear expectations, complete intake information, and no missed safety flags.

## Onboarding Workflow

1. Receive sales handoff.
2. Confirm payment and purchased offer.
3. Create or update client CRM record.
4. Assign coach.
5. Send onboarding message.
6. Collect intake form.
7. Confirm platform access.
8. Review intake for goals, constraints, and safety flags.
9. Schedule or request initial assessment inputs.
10. Set first coaching touchpoint.
11. Move client to `Onboarding Complete` when all required items are done.

## Required Sales Handoff

Coach must receive:

- Client name
- Email
- Phone if approved
- Segment
- Offer purchased
- Payment confirmation
- Primary goal
- Main obstacle
- Why now
- Training background
- Lifestyle constraints
- Injury considerations
- Sales expectations set
- Risk notes

If handoff is incomplete, request correction before programming.

## Onboarding Message

Use:

> Welcome to HTK. I am your coach inside the process. The first step is getting your intake and assessment information complete so I can understand your goal, training history, constraints, and what we need to account for. The more accurate you are up front, the better the plan can match where you are starting.

## Client Expectation Frame

Coach must communicate:

- HTK is performance coaching.
- The client must complete check-ins.
- The client must communicate issues early.
- The client must not train through concerning pain without telling the coach.
- Programming may be adjusted based on readiness and feedback.
- Results are not guaranteed.
- Medical questions must go to a qualified healthcare professional.

## Intake Completion Rules

Client cannot receive full active programming until:

- Intake form is complete.
- Goal is documented.
- Schedule constraints are documented.
- Equipment access is documented.
- Injury/safety questions are reviewed.
- Coach has enough baseline information to program responsibly.

## Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Sales handoff received.
- [ ] Payment confirmed.
- [ ] Coach assigned.
- [ ] CRM record complete.
- [ ] Welcome message sent.
- [ ] Intake form sent.
- [ ] Intake form completed.
- [ ] Platform access confirmed.
- [ ] Initial assessment requested or scheduled.
- [ ] Safety flags reviewed.
- [ ] First training block timeline communicated.

## Escalate During Onboarding If

- Client reports current injury and asks what to do.
- Client has not been medically cleared after serious issue.
- Client expects bodybuilding-only service.
- Client says sales promised something not in approved offer.
- Client requests refund, pause, discount, or exception.
- Client is hostile, abusive, or unsafe.

## Onboarding Completion Standard

Onboarding is complete when the coach can answer:

1. What is the client's primary performance goal?
2. What is the client's current training reality?
3. What constraints affect execution?
4. What safety flags exist?
5. What does the first training block need to accomplish?
