# 27. HTK Brand Protection Rules

## Objective

Protect HTK's reputation through accurate claims, professional coaching, safe escalation, and consistent client experience.

## Approved Claims

Coaches may say:

- HTK is performance coaching for people who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, athletic, durable, and capable in real life.
- HTK is built around athleticism and durability, not bodybuilding-only training.
- HTK coaching provides structure, accountability, and training adjustment.
- Results depend on execution, consistency, communication, recovery, and individual response.

## Prohibited Claims

Coaches must not say:

- HTK guarantees results.
- HTK prevents injury.
- HTK cures pain.
- HTK fixes medical conditions.
- HTK is physical therapy.
- HTK is bodybuilding coaching.
- Jimmy personally reviewed something unless he did.
- A client will achieve a specific result by a specific date unless approved and evidence-based.

## Brand Voice

HTK coaching sounds:

- Direct
- Professional
- Human
- Disciplined
- Performance-driven
- Calm

HTK coaching does not sound:

- Hype-driven
- Desperate
- Shame-based
- Aggressive
- Medical
- Overly familiar
- Corporate and empty

## Public Communication Rules

Coaches must not:

- Share client details publicly.
- Post before/after or testimonial without consent.
- Argue with clients or prospects online.
- Mock competitors.
- Make medical claims.
- Discuss private coaching conversations publicly.

## Client Privacy Rules

- Store client information only in approved systems.
- Share client information only with authorized HTK team members.
- Do not store passwords, payment information, medical records, or unnecessary private details.
- Keep CRM notes factual and professional.

## Escalate Brand Risk

Escalate immediately if:

- Client threatens public complaint.
- Client claims misrepresentation.
- Client asks for refund/charge issue.
- Client is public figure or media-sensitive.
- Client shares explicit, threatening, or abusive content.
- Coach is unsure how to respond.

## Final Message Test

Before sending a message, ask:

> Would HTK be comfortable if this exact message were shown publicly with the brand name attached?

If not clearly yes, rewrite or escalate.
