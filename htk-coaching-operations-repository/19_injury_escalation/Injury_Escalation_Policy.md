# 19. Injury Escalation Policy

## Objective

Protect the client, coach, and HTK brand when injury, pain, symptoms, or medical questions appear.

## Core Rule

HTK coaches do not diagnose, treat, rehab, or give medical advice. Coaches may modify training within scope and refer the client to a qualified healthcare professional when medical judgment is needed.

## Immediate Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately if the client reports:

- Acute injury.
- Sharp pain.
- Pain that changes movement.
- Numbness, tingling, radiating symptoms, or loss of function.
- Chest pain, faintness, severe shortness of breath, or concerning systemic symptoms.
- Head injury or suspected concussion.
- Medical diagnosis requiring training restrictions.
- Healthcare provider instructions the coach does not understand.
- Request for diagnosis, treatment, rehab, or medical interpretation.

## Injury Message Response

Use:

> I cannot give medical advice or diagnose that. Stop the movement that triggered it for now, and get guidance from a qualified healthcare professional if symptoms are concerning or continue. I am escalating this so we handle training appropriately.

## Injury Escalation Workflow

1. Stop giving programming advice for the injured area or concerning symptom.
2. Ask only operational questions needed for safety:
   - "Did this happen during training?"
   - "Have you been cleared to continue training?"
   - "Are there movements a healthcare professional told you to avoid?"
3. Tell client to seek qualified medical guidance when appropriate.
4. Notify manager.
5. Record objective facts in CRM.
6. Modify unaffected training only if safe and within scope.
7. Resume affected training only after clearance or manager-approved pathway.

## Allowed Coach Actions

Coach may:

- Pause an exercise.
- Substitute a non-painful movement pattern if no medical red flag exists.
- Reduce load, volume, range, complexity, or intensity.
- Program around unaffected areas when appropriate.
- Ask whether client has medical clearance.
- Document what client reports.

Coach may not:

- Diagnose.
- Treat.
- Prescribe rehab.
- Tell client to ignore pain.
- Interpret imaging or medical tests.
- Override healthcare provider guidance.
- Promise HTK can fix the issue.

## CRM Documentation Standard

Record:

- Date
- Client report
- Exercise or context if relevant
- Coach response
- Escalation sent
- Training modification
- Clearance status
- Next action

Use factual language only. Do not speculate.

## Return-To-Training Rules

Client may resume affected movement only when:

- Symptoms are resolved and no red flag exists, or
- Client reports clearance from qualified healthcare professional, or
- Manager-approved modification pathway is documented.

When returning:

- Start conservative.
- Use lower volume/intensity.
- Monitor response.
- Document every change.
