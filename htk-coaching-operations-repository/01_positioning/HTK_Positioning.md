# 1. HTK Positioning

## What HTK Is

Hard To Kill Training is premium performance coaching for men and high-output clients who want training that carries into real life.

HTK helps clients develop:

- Explosiveness
- Endurance
- Mobility
- Athleticism
- Functional strength
- Durability
- Real-world performance

HTK is for people who want to be capable, not merely tired. The purpose of training is to build a body that performs under demand.

## What HTK Is Not

HTK is not:

- Bodybuilding-only coaching
- A physique-first transformation challenge
- A random workout subscription
- A medical service
- Physical therapy
- Injury rehabilitation
- Pain diagnosis
- A place for clients who want results without accountability

Clients can care about appearance. Coaches should not shame that. But the coaching conversation must lead with performance, capability, durability, and training quality.

## Who HTK Serves

HTK serves:

- Athletes
- Former athletes
- Military
- Veterans
- First responders
- Performance-focused fitness enthusiasts
- High performers

Common client identity:

- Wants to feel athletic again.
- Wants strength and conditioning to work together.
- Wants to move better.
- Wants to be durable and capable.
- Wants structure and accountability.
- Has demanding work, sport, service, or life responsibilities.

## Core Client Promise

HTK provides coaching structure, performance-focused programming, accountability, and ongoing adjustment to help the client train toward real-world capability.

HTK does not guarantee results. Results depend on the client, consistency, communication, recovery, effort, and how the client's body responds.

## Coach Positioning Language

Use this when explaining HTK:

> HTK is performance coaching for people who want to be strong, conditioned, mobile, athletic, and capable in real life. It is built around athleticism and durability, not bodybuilding-only training.

Use this when a client asks why the plan includes mobility and conditioning:

> HTK is not just trying to make you better at one lift. The goal is to build a more capable body. Strength matters, but so do mobility, conditioning, movement quality, and durability.

Use this when a client wants appearance-only work:

> Looking better can be a byproduct of better training, consistency, and nutrition habits, but HTK is not a bodybuilding-only program. If performance is not part of the goal, this may not be the right fit.

Use this when setting standards:

> The program is only one part of the result. Execution, communication, sleep, recovery, and honesty in check-ins are part of the coaching process.

## Positioning Decision Rules

- If the client's goal includes performance, durability, conditioning, mobility, sport, readiness, or real-world capability, keep coaching within HTK.
- If the client wants bodybuilding-only outcomes, clarify whether they also value performance.
- If the client rejects performance and wants appearance-only programming, escalate to manager for fit review.
- If the client asks for medical treatment, diagnosis, rehab, pain interpretation, or injury protocols, follow the injury escalation and scope-of-practice SOPs.
