# 22. Retention & Renewal Process

## Objective

Retain good-fit clients by showing progress, setting the next performance target, and handling renewal before the client drifts.

## Retention Philosophy

Retention is earned through:

- Clear coaching
- Consistent communication
- Visible progress
- Accountability
- Professional handling of obstacles
- A next goal worth pursuing

Retention is not pressure.

## Renewal Review Timing

Start renewal review:

- 30 days before membership term ends, or
- At manager-defined renewal window, or
- When client asks about continuation, pause, or cancellation.

## Renewal Workflow

1. Review client progress.
2. Review adherence.
3. Review obstacles.
4. Identify next performance target.
5. Identify retention risk.
6. Prepare renewal recommendation.
7. Escalate high-risk cases to manager.
8. Conduct renewal conversation.
9. Record outcome in CRM.

## Renewal Conversation Structure

1. Recap starting point.
2. Recap progress.
3. Name remaining gap.
4. Present next performance target.
5. Ask client what they want next.
6. Recommend continuation only if fit remains.

Example:

> When you started, the main issue was inconsistent training and poor conditioning. Over this block, you completed [X] sessions and improved [marker]. The next gap is [gap]. If you want to keep building, the next focus should be [target].

## Retention Risk Flags

- Missed check-ins.
- Low adherence.
- No visible progress summary.
- Client confusion about plan.
- Client complaint.
- Client asks about cancellation.
- Client feels unsupported.
- Program does not match current goal.
- Coach has not documented progress.

## Decision Rules

Recommend renewal when:

- Client is a fit.
- Client is executing.
- Next goal is clear.
- Coaching continues to solve a real problem.

Do not push renewal when:

- Client is not executing and refuses accountability.
- Client needs medical clearance before training.
- Client wants a service HTK does not provide.
- Client is hostile or unsafe.
