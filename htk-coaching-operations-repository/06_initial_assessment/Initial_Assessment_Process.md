# 6. Initial Assessment Process

## Objective

Establish the client's starting point without diagnosing, treating, or overreaching outside coaching scope.

## Assessment Categories

1. Client profile
2. Training history
3. Goal category
4. Movement readiness
5. Strength baseline
6. Conditioning baseline
7. Mobility baseline
8. Recovery and lifestyle
9. Injury/safety flags
10. First-block recommendation

## Assessment Workflow

1. Review intake form.
2. Review sales handoff.
3. Confirm primary goal.
4. Confirm training age and background.
5. Review current weekly schedule.
6. Review equipment access.
7. Review movement or training videos if part of current HTK process.
8. Establish baseline performance markers.
9. Identify constraints and safety flags.
10. Decide whether client is cleared for standard programming, needs modification, or requires escalation.
11. Document assessment in CRM.

## Baseline Markers

Use markers relevant to the client's goal. Do not test everything.

Possible markers:

- Weekly training days completed
- Resting readiness note
- Strength movement baseline
- Conditioning benchmark
- Mobility limitation notes
- Jump/throw/sprint exposure level
- Work capacity
- Session RPE
- Recovery quality
- Bodyweight if relevant and approved

## Movement Readiness Review

Coach may assess:

- Exercise familiarity
- Movement control
- Range of motion needed for training
- Technical confidence
- Current exercise tolerance
- Need for regression or progression

Coach may not:

- Diagnose movement dysfunction as a medical issue.
- Claim a joint, tissue, or pathology is the cause.
- Prescribe rehab.
- Interpret pain medically.

## Decision Rules

### Standard Start

Use when:

- Intake complete.
- No concerning pain or medical restriction.
- Client can train consistently.
- Goal fits HTK.

Action:

- Build first training block.

### Modified Start

Use when:

- Client is deconditioned.
- Training history is limited.
- Schedule is constrained.
- Movement quality requires regression.
- Client is returning after time off but no medical red flag is present.

Action:

- Start with lower volume, simpler exercises, and conservative progression.

### Escalation Required

Use when:

- Client reports acute injury.
- Client asks for medical advice.
- Client reports symptoms that may require medical evaluation.
- Client has not been cleared to train after serious injury or medical event.
- Client reports pain that changes movement or daily function.

Action:

- Stop programming advice for that issue.
- Follow Injury Escalation Policy.

## Assessment Completion Checklist

- [ ] Intake reviewed.
- [ ] Goal category selected.
- [ ] Training history documented.
- [ ] Equipment documented.
- [ ] Schedule documented.
- [ ] Baseline markers selected.
- [ ] Mobility/conditioning/strength considerations noted.
- [ ] Safety flags reviewed.
- [ ] Start type selected: Standard, Modified, Escalation.
- [ ] CRM updated.
