# 2. Coach Role & Responsibilities

## Coach Mission

The HTK coach delivers the coaching experience after enrollment. The coach helps the client execute a performance-focused plan, communicate honestly, stay accountable, and progress safely within the coach's scope.

## Coach Responsibilities

The coach is responsible for:

1. Reviewing onboarding information.
2. Completing the initial assessment process.
3. Setting clear client goals and success markers.
4. Designing or assigning programming that matches HTK standards.
5. Adjusting training based on performance, readiness, constraints, and check-ins.
6. Reviewing weekly check-ins.
7. Tracking progress.
8. Maintaining client accountability.
9. Communicating clearly and professionally.
10. Escalating injuries, medical questions, brand-risk issues, and client-service concerns.
11. Updating the CRM.
12. Protecting HTK's coaching standard.

## What The Coach Does Not Do

The coach does not:

- Diagnose injuries.
- Treat pain.
- Provide physical therapy.
- Prescribe medical care.
- Give nutrition plans outside approved scope.
- Guarantee outcomes.
- Ignore safety flags.
- Debate clients emotionally.
- Shame clients.
- Make unapproved promises about refunds, extensions, discounts, or results.

## Daily Coach Workflow

1. Review new client assignments.
2. Review overdue onboarding items.
3. Review client messages.
4. Review check-ins due today.
5. Handle urgent escalations first.
6. Review training data and client notes.
7. Send required coaching responses.
8. Update programming only when a clear reason exists.
9. Update CRM.
10. Flag risks for manager.

## Weekly Coach Workflow

1. Review every active client's check-in.
2. Update progress markers.
3. Identify clients at risk of churn, non-compliance, injury, or disengagement.
4. Make programming adjustments.
5. Send coaching feedback.
6. Complete coach KPI report.
7. Submit escalation list.
8. Review quality sample with manager if assigned.

## Response Ownership

| Situation | Coach Action | Deadline |
|---|---|---:|
| New client assigned | Review handoff and onboarding | Same business day |
| Client check-in submitted | Review and respond | Within 1 business day |
| Client message | Respond or acknowledge | Within 1 business day |
| Injury/pain question | Stop coaching advice and escalate | Same day |
| Missed check-in | Follow missed check-in protocol | Same day due |
| Non-compliance pattern | Start accountability protocol | Within 1 business day |
| Refund/customer complaint | Escalate to manager | Same day |

## Coach Decision Authority

The coach may:

- Adjust exercise variations within HTK standards.
- Modify volume, intensity, rest, and progression based on client data.
- Regress exercises for quality and safety.
- Add mobility, conditioning, or readiness work aligned with the goal.
- Hold the client accountable to agreed actions.

The coach must escalate before:

- Pausing billing or discussing refunds.
- Recommending medical evaluation beyond general referral.
- Continuing to coach through concerning pain or injury.
- Making promises outside approved HTK terms.
- Removing a client from the program.
- Handling abusive or brand-risk communication.

## End-Of-Day Standard

Before signing off:

- Every urgent message is handled or escalated.
- Every check-in due today is reviewed or marked missed.
- Every program change has a note.
- Every safety concern is escalated.
- Every active client has a next action if needed.
- CRM reflects meaningful coaching actions.
