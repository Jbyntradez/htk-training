# 12. Explosive Performance Standards

## Objective

Develop power, speed, and athletic qualities through appropriate, high-quality explosive work.

## Explosive Work Categories

- Jumps
- Throws
- Bounds
- Low-level plyometrics
- Sprints
- Acceleration work
- Deceleration work
- Change-of-direction work
- Power-focused lifts when appropriate

## Readiness Requirements

Before explosive work, confirm:

- Client understands intent.
- Warm-up is complete.
- Movement quality is acceptable.
- Environment is safe.
- Fatigue is controlled.
- No pain or safety flag is present.

## Programming Rules

- Quality over volume.
- Stop when output or control drops.
- Use low dose at first.
- Progress complexity gradually.
- Match explosive work to goal.
- Do not add advanced plyometrics for novelty.
- Do not chase fatigue in explosive work.

## Progression Path

1. Low-level landing and control.
2. Low-amplitude jumps or throws.
3. Repeated quality reps.
4. Directional or reactive work.
5. Higher intensity or sport-specific exposure.

## Decision Rules

Add explosive work when:

- Goal requires athleticism, speed, power, sport, or readiness.
- Client demonstrates enough movement control.
- Recovery can support it.
- Environment is safe.

Regress explosive work when:

- Landings are loud, unstable, or uncontrolled.
- Client cannot maintain positions.
- Client reports pain.
- Fatigue reduces quality.
- Client is not recovering.

Remove or escalate when:

- Client reports acute pain.
- Client reports symptoms beyond normal exertion.
- Client lacks a safe training environment.
- Client has medical restrictions.

## Coaching Cues

Use simple cues:

- "Fast and clean."
- "Own the landing."
- "Stop before reps get sloppy."
- "Move with intent."
- "Quality first."

Avoid:

- Excessive cueing.
- Max-effort work before readiness.
- Conditioning disguised as power.
- High-volume jumps with poor landing quality.

## Explosive Performance Tracking

Track only relevant markers:

- Jump quality
- Throw intent
- Sprint exposure
- Landing control
- Perceived explosiveness
- Video review notes
- Output if measured
