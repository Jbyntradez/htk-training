# 21. Testimonials & Success Story SOP

## Objective

Collect client wins ethically, accurately, and with documented permission.

## Testimonial Eligibility

A client may be considered when:

- Client achieved meaningful progress.
- Client is satisfied with coaching.
- Client can describe the result honestly.
- Client has no active complaint or unresolved service issue.
- Coach can verify the claim with records or client statement.

## Success Story Categories

- Performance improvement
- Consistency improvement
- Conditioning improvement
- Mobility improvement
- Strength improvement
- Athletic confidence
- Return to training
- Better durability or work capacity
- Better adherence and accountability

## Testimonial Workflow

1. Identify potential client win.
2. Confirm CRM progress notes.
3. Ask manager for approval to request testimonial if required.
4. Send approved testimonial request.
5. Collect written/video response.
6. Confirm client consent for use.
7. Store testimonial and consent.
8. Tag CRM.
9. Submit to marketing/manager.

## Approved Request Message

Use:

> You have made real progress with [specific result]. If you are open to it, HTK may want to share your story to help other people understand what the coaching process looks like. No pressure either way. If you are open, I can send a few prompts and we will only use what you approve.

## Testimonial Prompts

Ask:

- What was your main goal when you started?
- What was not working before HTK?
- What changed during coaching?
- What result are you most proud of?
- What would you tell someone considering HTK?

## Claims Rules

Do not use testimonials that imply:

- Guaranteed results.
- Medical treatment.
- Injury cure.
- Unrealistic timeline.
- Bodybuilding-only transformation if not HTK positioning.
- Results that cannot be supported.

## Consent Requirements

Before use, record:

- Client name
- Date
- Testimonial content
- Approved usage format
- Whether name/photo/video can be used
- Any restrictions

## Escalate If

- Client mentions injury cure or medical claim.
- Client makes exaggerated claim.
- Client has active complaint.
- Client asks for compensation.
- Client is a public figure or sensitive case.
