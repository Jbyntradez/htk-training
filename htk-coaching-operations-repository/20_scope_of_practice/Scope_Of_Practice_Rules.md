# 20. Scope Of Practice Rules

## Objective

Define what HTK coaches may and may not do.

## HTK Coach Scope

Coaches may:

- Design performance-focused training.
- Adjust exercises, volume, intensity, and progression.
- Coach exercise execution.
- Provide accountability.
- Discuss training habits.
- Discuss recovery behaviors in general terms.
- Track progress.
- Refer clients to qualified professionals when needed.
- Communicate with manager about client risk.

## Outside Scope

Coaches may not:

- Diagnose medical conditions.
- Treat injuries.
- Prescribe rehab.
- Interpret pain medically.
- Interpret lab results, imaging, or medical tests.
- Prescribe medication or supplements as treatment.
- Give therapy or mental health counseling.
- Give legal, financial, or medical advice.
- Override medical restrictions.

## Nutrition Boundary

Unless HTK has an approved nutrition service and coach is authorized:

Coaches may discuss general habits:

- Protein consistency
- Hydration
- Meal consistency
- Fueling around training
- Sleep and recovery routines

Coaches may not:

- Prescribe medical nutrition therapy.
- Treat disease with diet.
- Create restrictive diets for medical conditions.
- Diagnose eating disorders.
- Manage supplements as medical treatment.

## Medical Referral Language

Use:

> That is outside my scope as a coach. You need to speak with a qualified healthcare professional for medical guidance. I can adjust training once we know what is appropriate.

## Scope Decision Rules

If the question is about training execution:

- Coach can answer.

If the question is about pain, diagnosis, treatment, rehab, medication, disease, or medical restrictions:

- Refer out and escalate if needed.

If the coach is unsure:

- Do not guess.
- Escalate to manager.

## Prohibited Phrases

Do not say:

- "You have [condition]."
- "This will fix your [injury]."
- "You do not need to see anyone."
- "Train through it."
- "That pain is normal" when symptoms are concerning.
- "I can rehab that."
- "Stop taking/starting medication."

## Coach Documentation Rule

Document scope issues with:

- Client question
- Coach response
- Referral or escalation
- Training change
- Next action

Never document speculation as fact.
