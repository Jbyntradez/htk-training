# 13. Client Communication Rules

## Objective

Keep communication clear, professional, accountable, and brand-safe.

## Communication Standards

HTK coach communication must be:

- Direct
- Professional
- Human
- Specific
- Performance-driven
- Calm
- Useful

It must not be:

- Vague
- Emotional
- Condescending
- Hype-driven
- Medical
- Overly familiar
- Aggressive
- Shame-based

## Response Time Standards

| Message Type | Standard |
|---|---:|
| Client weekly check-in | Within 1 business day |
| General client message | Within 1 business day |
| Urgent safety/injury message | Same business day escalation |
| Customer-service/refund complaint | Same business day escalation |
| Program clarification | Within 1 business day |

## Communication Workflow

1. Read the full client message.
2. Identify message type: training, check-in, schedule, pain/injury, accountability, customer service, brand risk.
3. Respond within coach scope.
4. Ask one clarifying question if needed.
5. Escalate if outside scope.
6. Log meaningful action in CRM.

## Approved Coaching Tone

Use:

> Good work getting the sessions in. The next priority is making the conditioning consistent, because that is the gap tied to your goal.

Use:

> If the schedule gets tight, do the minimum version instead of disappearing. Consistency beats an all-or-nothing week.

Use:

> I cannot give medical advice on that pain. Pause the movement for now and get appropriate medical guidance. I am escalating this so we handle it correctly.

## Do Not Say

- "Push through the pain."
- "That injury is probably nothing."
- "This will fix your knee/back/shoulder."
- "You are guaranteed to improve."
- "You failed."
- "No excuses" when the client reports a real constraint.
- "Jimmy wants you to..." unless Jimmy actually said it.

## Client Boundary Rules

- Do not debate politics, religion, or controversial topics.
- Do not flirt with clients.
- Do not share personal client information.
- Do not discuss other clients.
- Do not promise manager decisions.
- Do not handle refunds or disputes alone.
- Do not communicate in unapproved channels if HTK requires platform tracking.

## Message Escalation Triggers

Escalate when:

- Injury, pain, medical condition, or diagnosis is discussed.
- Client asks for rehab, treatment, or medical interpretation.
- Client is angry about payment, refund, or sales promise.
- Client threatens chargeback, legal action, or public complaint.
- Client is abusive, explicit, or unsafe.
- Client reports severe distress or emergency.

## Documentation Rule

Log any communication that changes:

- Program direction
- Goal
- Client compliance status
- Injury/safety status
- Renewal risk
- Customer-service status
- Escalation status
