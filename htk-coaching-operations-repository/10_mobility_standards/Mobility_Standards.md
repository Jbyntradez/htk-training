# 10. Mobility Standards

## Objective

Use mobility work to improve training positions, movement quality, durability, and performance readiness.

## Mobility Is Not Filler

Mobility work must have a purpose:

- Prepare for training.
- Improve usable range.
- Support better positions.
- Reduce movement friction.
- Reinforce control.
- Support durability.

## Mobility Assessment Areas

Review only what is relevant to the client's goal:

- Ankles
- Hips
- Thoracic spine
- Shoulders
- Hamstrings/posterior chain
- Adductors
- Breathing/bracing
- Squat/lunge/hinge positions
- Overhead positions when relevant

## Mobility Workflow

1. Identify movement limitation affecting training.
2. Select 1-3 target areas.
3. Assign simple mobility drills.
4. Pair mobility with training movement when possible.
5. Track whether movement quality improves.
6. Adjust or remove drills that do not create useful change.

## Decision Rules

Add mobility when:

- Client cannot access needed positions.
- Warm-up quality is poor.
- Stiffness limits performance.
- Training goal requires better range or control.
- Client reports repeated movement restriction without medical red flag.

Do not use mobility to:

- Treat pain.
- Diagnose injury.
- Replace medical care.
- Fill time because the program lacks structure.
- Avoid strength or conditioning work.

## Mobility Programming Standards

- Keep drills simple.
- Use clear instructions.
- Limit the number of drills.
- Connect drills to the workout.
- Track client response.
- Progress from passive access to active control when appropriate.

## Mobility Check-In Questions

- "What felt restricted this week?"
- "Did mobility work improve your training positions?"
- "Which drill felt useful?"
- "Which drill felt like it did not change anything?"
- "Did any drill create pain or symptoms?"

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate if:

- Client reports sharp pain.
- Client reports numbness, tingling, or radiating symptoms.
- Mobility drill creates concerning symptoms.
- Client asks for diagnosis.
- Client says a healthcare provider restricted movement.

## Mobility Success Markers

Possible markers:

- Better exercise setup.
- Better depth or range with control.
- Less warm-up time needed.
- Improved movement confidence.
- Better recovery perception.
- More consistent training execution.
